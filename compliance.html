Compliance crosswalk

This page provides an informational mapping between NADIR’s artifact mechanics and common accountability obligations and risk frameworks. It is not legal advice, not a compliance certification, and not a representation that any deployment is compliant. Compliance determinations are made by deployers, auditors, and relevant authorities.

Enter crosswalk Document suite
[INTERPRETATION]

What this page is and is not

Procurement reviewers evaluate claims. This crosswalk stays within verifiable mechanics and bounded statements. It explains how NADIR can support record-keeping and review workflows that appear in modern governance regimes, without claiming that NADIR makes an operator compliant.

[IS]

A mechanics mapping

A structured mapping between NADIR features (policy scope, canonicalization, integrity sealing, disclosure tiers, verification) and common accountability requirements (record-keeping, provenance, reviewability, audit trails).

[IS NOT]

A compliance claim

Not a statement that NADIR or any deployer is compliant with any law or framework. Compliance depends on the deployer’s system, processes, risk controls, documentation, and lawful basis.

[AUDIENCE]

Reviewers and counsel

Written for procurement officers, security reviewers, privacy reviewers, and counsel. Language remains conservative, avoids superlatives, and maintains explicit boundaries.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR produces integrity-sealed artifacts under policy; institutions and operators make determinations.
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, correctness, or evidentiary admissibility.
  • All mappings are informational and must be validated for the specific deployment and jurisdiction.
[EU AI ACT]

Record-keeping and transparency support

EU AI governance regimes commonly require record-keeping, transparency to deployers, and documentation posture for higher-risk systems. NADIR’s artifact layer can support those obligations by producing bounded, integrity-sealed records. Specific legal requirements must be evaluated by deployers and counsel.

[RECORD-KEEPING]

Integrity-bound logging posture

Policy-scoped capture and canonical artifacts provide a structured alternative to ad-hoc telemetry. Hashing and signatures support tamper-evidence and later verification under defined boundaries.

[TRANSPARENCY]

Deployers and reviewers

Disclosure tiers allow deployers to provide reviewability and verification to authorized audiences without exposing proprietary weights, architecture internals, or confidential code by default.

[DOCUMENTATION]

Schema and policy versioning

Artifacts can include Policy ID, policy version, policy hash, canonicalization version, and environment fingerprints to support documentation traceability and change control across deployments.

[DISCIPLINE]

NADIR does not assert that any specific article, annex, or conformity assessment criterion is satisfied by default. NADIR provides record infrastructure that may support deployers in meeting record-keeping and transparency duties when configured appropriately and paired with governance controls, documentation, and lawful processes.

[NIST AI RMF]

Function-level alignment

Modern risk frameworks separate governance, mapping, measurement, and management. NADIR supports evidence generation for review and accountability processes. The mapping below is informational and does not represent certification or conformance.

[GOVERN]

Policies, roles, oversight

Capture policies, masking rules, disclosure tiers, policy identifiers, and versioning support governance controls and internal oversight structures. NADIR records which policy governed each artifact.

[MAP]

Context and boundaries

Policy-scoped capture defines what is inside and outside the record boundary. Canonical artifact fields establish consistent semantics for what is being recorded and later reviewed.

[MEASURE]

Integrity signals, not performance claims

Verification produces integrity outcomes (PASS/FAIL, reason codes). NADIR does not measure model accuracy, fairness, or safety. It measures whether a record matches its integrity claims under policy.

[MANAGE]

Incident workflows and change control

Policy versioning and artifact lineage can support operational response workflows, audit trails, and post-incident reconstruction. Incident response and reporting obligations remain the deployer’s responsibility.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR supports accountability workflows by producing verifiable records; it does not manage risk by itself.
  • Any framework mapping must be validated against the deployer’s system, process controls, and documentation.
[PROCUREMENT SCRUTINY]

Statements reviewers look for

Procurement reviewers expect explicit statements about determinism, resource isolation, cryptographic agility, and key custody. These statements are phrased conservatively and refer to configuration and roadmap where appropriate.

[DETERMINISM]

Canonicalization before hashing

For JSON artifacts, deterministic canonicalization (e.g., RFC 8785 where specified) is applied prior to hashing to avoid “brittle hash” failures due to non-semantic serialization differences.

[AVAILABILITY]

Back-pressure controls

Where deployed as a sidecar, resource isolation may be enforced via containerization and quota controls (e.g., cgroups) to reduce the risk that the artifact layer consumes resources reserved for the inference pathway.

[CRYPTO AGILITY]

PQC readiness posture

NADIR supports cryptographic agility by design. Signature and hashing suites are deployment-defined. A phased PQC readiness roadmap may be provided under controlled review to support future transitions where required.

[KEY CUSTODY POINTER]

Key custody is a governance decision and must be explicit for institutional review. NADIR’s intended posture is non-custodial: signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment (e.g., HSM/KMS). NADIR does not need to possess or transmit private keys to support verification workflows. See Key Custody.

[STATE DISCLOSURES]

Jurisdiction-specific posture

State-level AI governance evolves. NADIR does not publish blanket claims about jurisdictional compliance. Where required, NADIR can provide policy-scoped artifact and documentation support under controlled review for a specific deployment and legal basis.

[DISCIPLINED STATEMENT]

Any state-level disclosure or reporting obligation is satisfied by the deployer’s governance program and lawful processes. NADIR can support those programs by producing integrity-sealed records, verification outputs, and policy versioning metadata consistent with the deployer’s requirements and authorized reviewer workflows.

For procurement and institutional review, NADIR can provide a deployment-specific crosswalk and statutory alignment log under controlled review, including which policy version corresponds to which governance requirement at the time of deployment.
