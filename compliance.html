Compliance Center Summary

CEYO can support oversight workflows by producing policy-scoped artifacts that are canonical, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable under constrained disclosure tiers. Determinations remain institutional.

Mechanics Determinism

Boundary Non-adjudication

Capture Minimization

Keys Non-custodial

DESCRIPTIVE CROSSWALK

Mapping Evidence Mechanics
To Common Requirements

The following crosswalk is illustrative. It is intended to help oversight teams understand where CEYO can support verification and review workflows. It is not a claim of compliance or adequacy.
Requirement Theme CEYO Capability (Descriptive) Notes / Boundary
Auditability
 Canonical artifacts (stable schema + deterministic bytes) support repeatable review and independent recomputation. Auditability here refers to evidence mechanics, not correctness of the underlying model or policy compliance outcomes.
Integrity & Provenance
 Hashing + digital signatures establish tamper-evidence and origin authenticity for the artifact under policy scope. Signature trust depends on institutional key management and authorization. CEYO does not hold private signing keys.
Minimization
 Policy-defined inclusion/exclusion/masking rules declare what is captured before generation; policy version is embedded. CEYO does not imply collection is “necessary” or “lawful.” Institutions define scope and purpose.
Controlled Disclosure
 Tiered disclosure (Public / Redacted / Controlled Review) supports verification without default exposure of proprietary internals. Disclosure is a process and authorization model. Public pages are descriptive; specifics are shared under controlled conditions.
Operational Continuity
 Fail-open posture preserves inference continuity; sealing failure yields marked artifacts and logged events. Fail-open reduces operational coupling but does not prevent deployment errors or misuse; it improves observability.
Separation of Roles
 Verification confirms integrity/provenance. Determinations about compliance, legality, fairness, and admissibility remain institutional. CEYO is infrastructure; it does not adjudicate outcomes or enforce policy.
Boundary Declaration · V2026.02.19

CEYO produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance outcomes. Authorized institutions retain responsibility for determinations.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope — not correctness of model output. CEYO does not certify regulatory compliance and does not guarantee legal admissibility.
