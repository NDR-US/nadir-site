Compliance Crosswalk

This page provides a structured mapping between NADIR’s evidentiary mechanics and common regulatory expectations for logging, transparency, governance, and incident response. It is a posture reference, not legal advice, and does not represent certification or compliance status.

Enter crosswalk Trust Center
[EU POSTURE]

Logging and transparency support posture

NADIR is designed to produce policy-scoped, integrity-sealed records that may support deployer logging and transparency obligations for regulated AI systems. Exact obligations depend on system classification, role (provider/deployer), and jurisdictional implementation.

[LOGGING]

Record-keeping mechanics

Policy-defined capture converts selected inference context into canonical artifacts, then seals them cryptographically to support integrity verification and later reconstruction under oversight.

[TRANSPARENCY]

Deployer interpretability

Artifacts can be structured with tiered disclosure so deployers and reviewers can interpret provenance, applied policy scope, and integrity status without default exposure of proprietary internals.

[DOCUMENTATION]

Technical documentation support

NADIR’s controlled documentation suite is intended to provide implementation details, capture schemas, and verification contracts suitable for formal review when disclosure is authorized.

[BOUNDARY]
  • This crosswalk is descriptive and non-exhaustive; it does not constitute legal advice or a representation of compliance.
  • Deployers remain responsible for determining regulatory classification, implementing required controls, and maintaining documentation.
  • NADIR provides verifiable records; authorized institutions determine oversight conclusions.
[NIST AI RMF]

Governance-oriented controls mapping

NADIR is designed to support risk governance by producing integrity-bound artifacts and verification workflows. Mapping below is functional: it describes where NADIR can support operational practices, not where it replaces them.

[STATE POSTURE]

State-level transparency and incident response posture

U.S. state AI requirements vary and evolve. NADIR’s posture is to support verifiable record generation, controlled disclosure, and incident reconstruction workflows that may assist deployers in meeting transparency and reporting obligations.

[DISCLOSURE]

Public vs. controlled tiers

Public-facing outputs can remain intentionally high-level, while controlled review can provide expanded fields under authorization.

[INCIDENTS]

72-hour workflow support

A structured playbook can help teams assemble verified summaries and retrieval packages. Reporting duties remain with deployers.

[RETENTION]

Deployment-defined retention

NADIR does not prescribe retention periods. Implementing organizations define retention consistent with law and policy.

[NOTE]

If you publish state-specific claims (named statutes, deadlines, or mandates), keep them in a versioned appendix and update them as laws change. Avoid marketing superlatives. Use only verifiable, bounded language.

[VERSIONED ALIGNMENT]

Statutory alignment log and hard-truth clauses

Procurement reviewers look for operational and legal separation: deterministic canonicalization, resource isolation, and non-custodial key posture. These clauses are included here as explicit, reviewable statements.

[STABILIZATION]

Canonicalization standard

NADIR’s verification contract can specify deterministic canonicalization for JSON artifacts (e.g., RFC 8785) prior to hashing, to support integrity checks across heterogeneous environments.

[BACK-PRESSURE]

Resource isolation posture

NADIR is designed to support strict sidecar isolation and resource quotas (e.g., cgroup-based limits), preventing the audit layer from consuming CPU or memory reserved for core inference pathways.

[KEY CUSTODY]

Non-custodial design intent

NADIR is designed for non-custodial deployments: signing keys are generated and retained within the client’s controlled environment (HSM/KMS or equivalent). NADIR does not require possession of client private keys.

[PQC READINESS]

Cryptographic suites evolve. NADIR’s posture is to support cryptographic agility and a roadmap for post-quantum readiness where required by institutional standards, procurement profiles, or future regulatory guidance. Specific algorithm choices belong in controlled technical documentation.
