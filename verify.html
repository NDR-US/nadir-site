Verification

Independent Integrity Checks and Declared Review Context

CEYO verification evaluates whether an artifact remains structurally valid, deterministically intact, and attributable to the declared sealing context. It concerns later review of the artifact itself, not model intervention, adjudication, or retraining.

Verification focus

Structure, integrity recomputation, provenance validation, and risk-oriented institutional review without model interference.

Verification Model

Verification asks whether the artifact still means what it claimed to mean at sealing time.

Verification is not a re-run of the originating system. It is an independent evaluation of the artifact against the deterministic rules, integrity state, and provenance context declared when the artifact was sealed.

That means the core verification question is narrow and disciplined: does the artifact remain structurally valid, internally consistent, and attributable to the declared sealing authority?

Review proceeds in a disciplined order.

01

Interpret the artifact

Read the artifact according to the protocol structure it claims to follow.

02

Rebuild deterministic form

Apply canonicalization rules to reconstruct the stable representation required for integrity evaluation.

03

Recompute integrity

Derive the expected digest again and compare it to the sealed artifact state.

04

Evaluate provenance

Assess whether the signature context remains consistent with the declared key authority and sealing claim.

Governance Surface

Deterministic artifacts across the governance surface.

CEYO does not interfere with model execution; it operates as an independent layer that seals policy-scoped inference artifacts at the moment they are declared. That gives institutions a deterministic, audit-ready record that can later be evaluated against governance concerns such as bias, security, privacy, robustness, and procedural control.

Risk Radar Chart

Verification does not score the model or decide whether a governance concern has been resolved. It preserves the sealed evidence needed for later institutional review across the governance surface, without modifying the model itself.

Independent Review

Verification happens outside the model.

Verification is designed to occur outside the model and outside the original runtime environment.

That separation matters. It allows an institution, counterparty, or downstream reviewer to evaluate the integrity and provenance of the artifact without requiring direct access to the full internal mechanics of the originating system.

This is why CEYO operates as an independent evidentiary layer rather than as a model-internal monitoring function.

External posture

The reviewer evaluates the artifact from outside the originating system boundary.

No model interference

Verification does not require changing model weights or reopening runtime execution.

Portable review

The artifact remains evaluable when paired with the declared verification context.

Verification Outcomes

Verification can confirm artifact integrity, but it does not resolve every institutional question.

Structure holds

The artifact remains legible and internally stable under protocol interpretation.

Integrity holds

The recomputed deterministic state remains consistent with the sealed digest.

Provenance holds

The signature and declared key context remain attributable to the sealing claim.

Limits

Verification is narrower than adjudication.

Verification does not decide whether the underlying decision was substantively correct, legally sufficient, or normatively justified.

It determines whether the sealed artifact remains structurally sound, deterministically intact, and attributable to the declared sealing context. Those are necessary conditions for review, but they are not the whole of judgment.

What verification can confirm

That the artifact structure is valid, that deterministic integrity holds, and that provenance remains consistent with the declared sealing context.

What verification cannot confirm

That the originating decision was true, fair, compliant, or institutionally sufficient in every downstream sense.
