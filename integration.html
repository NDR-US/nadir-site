Deployment Model

Three Attachment Patterns

CEYO may be deployed as a sidecar, gateway, or wrapper depending on infrastructure architecture, latency tolerance, and institutional security requirements.

All patterns preserve a single principle: the inference engine remains unchanged. The integrity layer operates externally.

01 / Sidecar Co-located
Runs alongside the inference service (container/pod). Suitable for Kubernetes environments. Minimizes pipeline disruption while producing parallel artifacts.
02 / Gateway Boundary proxy
Positioned between calling system and inference endpoint. Useful where traffic control, routing, and network-layer fail-open enforcement are required.
03 / Wrapper Interface-compatible
Wraps inference calls to preserve interface compatibility. Useful for legacy integrations or environments requiring controlled orchestration without altering model internals.
Configuration Before Operation

Policy Defines Scope

Before artifacts are generated, operators define capture policy. CEYO does not default to broad collection. Scope must be explicit.

Policy versioning is mandatory. Any change to scope requires a new policy version. Artifacts reference the policy version in effect at time of capture.

01 / Scope Included / excluded
Define which inference events and contextual fields are captured, which are excluded by design, and which are masked or hashed before canonicalization.
02 / Disclosure Tiered access
Configure Public/Redacted and Controlled Review tiers. Disclosure boundaries are defined before artifact generation to avoid post hoc ambiguity.
03 / Policy hash Optional posture
Where configured, include a cryptographic digest of the policy document within the artifact to strengthen alignment claims against post hoc policy modification.
Artifact Lifecycle

Generate, Retain, Retrieve

CEYO produces artifacts at inference time. Storage, retention, and access control are defined by the operator to align with privacy law, regulatory requirements, and governance frameworks.

Chain-of-custody handling after artifact generation remains an institutional responsibility. CEYO provides sealing and review-ready structure.

01 / Storage Operator-defined
Store artifacts in access-controlled repositories. Retrieval events should be logged. Tamper-evidence is supported through deterministic structure and cryptographic sealing.
02 / Retention Policy aligned
Retention duration is not prescribed by CEYO. Operators define retention consistent with applicable privacy law and regulatory obligations.
03 / Review readiness Institutional posture
Artifacts are structured for verification summaries, policy alignment checks, and controlled disclosure packaging suitable for formal review workflows.
Operational Performance

Fail-Open Continuity Discipline

CEYO is designed to introduce minimal additional latency per inference event. Overhead depends on capture scope, cryptographic suite, hardware, and storage configuration.

In fail-open posture, inference continues even if artifact sealing fails. Failures are logged and become reviewable integrity events.

01 / Latency Scope-dependent
Performance overhead is proportional to capture surface area and cryptographic operations. Benchmark methodology is shared under controlled review; public pages do not claim universal figures.
02 / Fail-open Not a choke point
CEYO must not become a single point of failure. If the integrity layer faults, inference continues uninterrupted unless explicitly configured otherwise by the operator.
03 / Concurrency Determinism preserved
Scaling must preserve deterministic output. Concurrency must not introduce nondeterministic ordering that affects canonical bytes or digest outcomes.
