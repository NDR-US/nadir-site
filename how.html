Mechanics chain · policy scope · deterministic verification

How CEYO works.

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary and produces integrity-sealed artifacts under explicit policy scope.

The mechanism is intentionally narrow: capture only what has been declared, canonicalize it deterministically, seal it cryptographically, and make it independently verifiable without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

Mechanism summary Capture → Canonicalize → Seal → Verify
  • Operators define capture scope before artifacts are generated.
  • Only declared fields are included; exclusions and masking occur before sealing.
  • Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope, not decision correctness.

Sequence

Five operational stages.

CEYO is not an open-ended telemetry layer. It follows a fixed evidentiary sequence with declared boundaries at each stage.

Stage logic

Attach

Place CEYO at a defined boundary where inference-time events can be observed without modifying model weights by default.

Capture

Collect only policy-scoped fields. Anything excluded or masked is handled before artifact creation.

Canonicalize

Produce deterministic serialized bytes so verification does not depend on implementation quirks, formatting differences, or output ordering.

Seal

Apply hashing and digital signatures to make post-seal alteration detectable and provenance reviewable.

Verify

Allow an independent party to reproduce the validation path without requiring default exposure of proprietary internals.

Deployment posture

Where CEYO sits in the stack.

CEYO is designed to operate beside inference workflows, not as a replacement for decision authority and not as a requirement to expose model internals by default.

Pattern What it means Why it may be used Boundary
Sidecar CEYO runs adjacent to an application or model-serving process. Local capture posture with clearer operational separation. No modification of model weights by default.
Gateway CEYO observes requests and responses at a controlled ingress or egress boundary. Centralized evidentiary control across multiple downstream systems. Scope remains limited to gateway-visible policy fields.
Wrapper CEYO encapsulates a call boundary around an inference operation. Explicit integration path for teams that want a narrow evidentiary layer. Still policy-scoped, not a general telemetry feed.
Availability posture

CEYO can support fail-open deployment posture. If sealing fails, inference continuity can remain independent while the event is flagged or logged for later review.

That means artifact generation can support oversight without becoming the default operational gate unless an operator deliberately configures it that way.

Verification model

What reviewers actually do.

Independent verification is mechanical. It does not depend solely on trusting the producer’s narrative about what happened.

Verification sequence

1 · Canonicalize

Re-run the declared canonicalization procedure to reproduce the byte representation used for sealing.

2 · Recompute hash

Calculate the digest over canonical bytes and compare the result to the artifact’s declared digest.

3 · Validate signature

Use the public key reference or trusted key chain to confirm that the signature matches the declared digest.

4 · Confirm policy alignment

Check that policy ID, policy version, disclosure tier, and other declared policy metadata match expected values.

What verification does not prove

Not decision correctness

Integrity of record is not the same as correctness of model output or operator judgment.

Not legal sufficiency

Acceptance in any judicial, regulatory, or internal proceeding is determined by the relevant authority.

Not full replay of all conditions

Verification confirms the declared artifact path, not universal replay of every inference condition beyond scope.

Boundary declaration Integrity · Provenance · Scope
  • CEYO produces integrity-sealed records.
  • Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope.
  • CEYO does not certify compliance, legality, fairness, or correctness of model output.
