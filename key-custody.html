Key custody declaration

NADIR is designed to operate in a non-custodial signing posture. In typical deployments, private signing keys are generated, stored, and retained within the client-controlled environment (HSM/KMS/TEE as configured). NADIR does not require possession of client private keys to produce verifiable artifacts.

View custody model Document suite
[DECLARATION]

Non-custodial architecture

Key custody determines who can create signatures and, therefore, who can plausibly forge artifacts. Institutional buyers typically require separation between platform operator and signing authority.

[KEY ORIGIN]

Generated in client boundary

In typical configurations, private keys are generated inside client-controlled HSM/KMS/TEE. Key material does not leave the client boundary in plaintext.

[SIGNING]

Remote signing calls

NADIR submits canonical artifact digests for signing using the client’s configured signing service. NADIR receives signatures, not private keys.

[VERIFICATION]

Public key distribution

Verification relies on public keys or certificates distributed via the client’s trust policy (PKI, pinned keys, or agreed trust anchors).

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR does not require custodial access to client private keys.
  • Actual custody posture depends on deployment architecture and contractual terms.
  • Key custody does not determine legal admissibility; admissibility is determined by the relevant authority.
[MODELS]

Typical custody patterns

Deployment-specific custody patterns are selected to match regulatory requirements, risk posture, and operational constraints.

[CLIENT HSM/KMS]

Client-controlled signing

Keys generated and stored in client HSM or cloud KMS. Signing is performed via client policy. NADIR integrates as a caller under least privilege.

[TEE ATTESTATION]

Attested signer boundary

Signing performed inside an attested enclave boundary. Verification may incorporate attestation evidence per deployment policy.

[CONTROLLED REVIEW NOTE]

Detailed implementation diagrams, key policy schemas, and operational runbooks are available under controlled review and are not fully disclosed on the public site.

[LIFECYCLE]

Rotation, revocation, continuity

Institutional workflows require continuity across key rotation events and an auditable revocation posture when compromise is suspected.

[ROTATION]

Planned rotation

Rotation schedules and policies are set by the operator. Verification maintains continuity through key identifiers, certificate chains, or pinned public keys as configured.

[REVOCATION]

Compromise response

Revocation status is evaluated by the verification policy. Artifacts signed prior to revocation remain subject to institutional interpretation and applicable legal standards.

[LOGGING]

Lifecycle trace

Key events (creation, rotation, revocation) may be logged under the operator’s security program. NADIR does not replace existing security governance.

[PQC READINESS]

Cryptographic suites may evolve. Deployment policies may include a forward-compatibility posture (cryptographic agility) to support future transitions, including post-quantum approaches, as standards and institutional requirements mature.
