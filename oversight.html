Oversight Portal

NADIR enables third-party verification of integrity-bound artifacts without default disclosure of model weights, proprietary architecture, or trade-secret implementation detail. Oversight is policy-scoped, role-based, and auditable.

Enter oversight model PCL engineering
[ACCESS MODEL]

Role-based oversight without IP exposure

NADIR separates operational control (the deployer) from verification authority (the institution). Oversight access is granted by policy scope and authorization, not by default telemetry access.

[OPERATOR]

Regulated deployer

Configures policy scope, runs the inference system, and retains artifacts in the deployer-controlled environment.

[INSTITUTION]

Authorized reviewer

Verifies specific artifacts and integrity proofs without receiving weights, training data, or confidential architecture.

[AUDITOR]

Forensic examiner

Performs post-incident reconstruction using sealed records, subject to the same policy-scoped disclosure constraints.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR provides evidence infrastructure, not judgments about correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance.
  • Authorization and scope govern what can be reviewed; NADIR does not grant oversight authority.
  • Verification confirms integrity and provenance of an artifact, not the truth of a model output.
[WORKFLOW]

Verification workflow

Oversight is performed against deterministic artifacts: canonicalized structure, cryptographic sealing, and traceable custody.

[SELECT]

Choose decision state

Reviewer requests a specific event or decision-state artifact by ID or time range under granted scope.

[VERIFY]

Validate integrity proof

Verify canonicalization, hash chain, timestamping, and signature status against the stated custody model.

[EXPORT]

Produce a review packet

Generate a policy-scoped packet suitable for institutional review, escalation, or incident reporting workflows.

[DISCLOSURE CONTROL]
  • Policy-scoped inclusion/exclusion and masking rules are applied before review packets are generated.
  • PII handling follows explicit scope and redaction rules aligned to the PCL Engineering posture.
  • Artifacts can be verified even when portions are redacted, using integrity-preserving representations.
