Oversight Portal

The oversight posture is designed for institutional review under constrained disclosure. Authorized reviewers can verify integrity and policy alignment of specific artifacts without default exposure of proprietary weights, confidential architecture, or unrelated operational data.

[ROLE-BASED OVERSIGHT]

Institutional access without proprietary exposure

NADIR is designed to support an “air-gap” between operator infrastructure and external review: disclosure is tiered by policy, access is authorized, and verification focuses on integrity and provenance—not adjudication.

[OPERATORS]

Regulated operators

Define policy scope, retention posture, disclosure tiers, and escalation thresholds under internal governance and legal obligations.

[INSTITUTIONS]

Authorized institutions

Verify integrity, provenance, and policy alignment for specific artifacts under controlled review—without default access to trade secrets.

[AUDITORS]

Forensic reviewers

Perform incident reconstruction and chain-of-custody workflow checks using verified artifact packages and retrieval logs.

[BOUNDARY]
  • Oversight workflows validate integrity and provenance under policy; they do not certify correctness, legality, or compliance status.
  • Disclosure and access are defined by the implementing organization and applicable jurisdictional requirements.
  • Admissibility and evidentiary weight are determined by the relevant authority.
[POLICY-SCOPED REDACTION]

Constrained disclosure with verifiable integrity

Masking and disclosure are policy-defined. The objective is to enable verification while minimizing unnecessary exposure of personal data, sensitive operational details, or proprietary implementation information.

[MINIMIZATION]

Field selection

Policies define included fields, excluded fields, and masking rules. Unnecessary collection is explicitly excluded by design.

[REDACTION]

Tiered visibility

Disclosure tiers can be structured so reviewers receive only what is necessary for integrity verification and oversight tasks.

[PROVENANCE]

Policy binding

Artifacts include policy identifiers, versions, and hashes so verification can confirm which scope rules governed capture.

[PURPOSE LIMITATION]

Audit artifacts are generated for defined oversight and reconstruction purposes under policy scope. Repurposing artifacts for unrelated surveillance, secondary use, or other non-authorized purposes should be governed by explicit institutional authorization, applicable law, and the operator’s policy constraints.

[VERIFICATION WORKFLOW]

Deterministic checks, pass/fail outputs

Verification recomputes integrity values over canonicalized artifacts, validates signatures, and confirms policy alignment. Verification confirms integrity and provenance—not correctness, safety, or legal compliance.

[KEY CUSTODY]

NADIR is designed for non-custodial deployments: signing keys are generated and retained within the client’s controlled environment (HSM/KMS or equivalent); NADIR does not require possession of client private keys to produce verifiable records. Key management and custody policies are defined by the implementing organization.
