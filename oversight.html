Oversight Portal

NADIR is designed to enable institutional verification of integrity-sealed artifacts without default exposure of model weights, proprietary architecture, or trade-secret implementation. The Oversight Portal describes verification workflows and role-scoped access patterns used under controlled review.

Enter portal model PCL controls
[ACCESS TIERS]

Role-based oversight

Oversight is designed around constrained disclosure. Roles determine what can be viewed, what can be verified, and what remains masked under policy scope.

[TIERS]
Regulated operators
Configure Policy Scope, retention, residency, and internal access controls. Operators remain responsible for deployment decisions and lawful use.
Authorized institutions
Verify integrity and provenance of specific artifacts under constrained disclosure. Verification does not require default access to model weights or proprietary architecture.
Forensic auditors
Reconstruct incident timelines using custody-ready artifacts, hashes, signatures, and policy scope logs. Access is role-scoped and jurisdiction-sensitive.
[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR produces integrity-bound records. It does not adjudicate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance outcomes.
  • Authorized institutions and operators make determinations and retain responsibility for decisions.
  • Policy Scope governs inclusion, exclusion, masking, access tiers, and retention.
[WORKFLOW]

Verification without IP exposure

The portal’s primary function is to allow a reviewer to verify that an artifact was generated under defined policy scope and has not been altered, while minimizing disclosure of sensitive fields.

[STEP 01]

Select an artifact identifier

A reviewer begins with a single artifact ID, timestamp window, or incident case reference. Review is initiated under controlled review procedures defined by the operator and authorized institution.

[STEP 02]

Confirm policy scope and masking

The portal surfaces the policy scope version associated with the artifact, including inclusion/exclusion rules and masking directives. Sensitive fields may be redacted while preserving cryptographic verifiability.

[STEP 03]

Verify deterministic sealing

JSON artifacts are canonicalized prior to hashing (RFC 8785 for JSON artifacts), then hashed and signed. Verification confirms the signature over the canonical form to support tamper-evidence.

[STEP 04]

Produce a verification record

The output of review is a verification record (hash, signature status, scope version, timestamp bounds, and reviewer attestations as applicable). The portal is designed to support later reconstruction and institutional handling.

[KEY CUSTODY]

NADIR is designed for non-custodial key posture: signing keys are generated and retained within the operator’s controlled environment (e.g., HSM/KMS). NADIR does not require possession or transmission of private keys for verification workflows.

[CONSTRAINED DISCLOSURE NOTE]

The portal is designed so that institutional verification can be performed over canonicalized artifacts even when content is partially redacted under policy scope. This is intended to reduce unnecessary exposure while preserving integrity verification.

[OPERATIONAL]

Integrity–availability balance

High-stakes environments may require explicit posture about audit continuity versus inference continuity. The portal documents expected behavior under defined operator configuration.

[FAIL-OPEN / FAIL-CLOSED]

NADIR is designed so that audit capture failure does not necessarily imply operational inference failure. Operators define whether the deployment is configured to fail-open (continue inference with audit degradation) or fail-closed (halt under integrity failure) based on risk posture and governance requirements.

[RESOURCE ISOLATION]

Where applicable, NADIR may be deployed with resource isolation controls (e.g., cgroup-based quotas) to reduce risk of operational back-pressure on the inference engine. Exact constraints and performance targets are deployment-specific and documented under controlled review.
