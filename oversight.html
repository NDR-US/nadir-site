Oversight portal

NADIR supports institutional review workflows by providing integrity-sealed artifacts under controlled disclosure. Oversight verifies provenance and tamper-resistance under policy without requiring exposure of client proprietary internals. This page describes the access model and verification workflow; it does not represent an operational deployment or a live product dashboard.

[ACCESS MODEL]

Role-based oversight boundaries

NADIR separates operational control from review authority. The deployer operates the inference system; authorized reviewers validate artifact integrity and policy alignment under a constrained disclosure posture. Access is policy-defined and can be layered by audience, mandate, and agreement.

[DEPLOYER]

Regulated operators

Configure capture policy, masking rules, disclosure tiers, retention posture, and verification trust anchors within their governance environment. Maintain custody and accountability for operations.

[REVIEWER]

Authorized institutions

Verify hashes, signatures, timestamps (if configured), and policy identifiers. Review artifacts under constrained disclosure without default access to weights, architecture internals, or proprietary source code.

[AUDITOR]

Forensic auditors

Support incident reconstruction by validating integrity and provenance, correlating artifacts to case timelines, and producing review-ready outputs consistent with chain-of-custody workflows defined by the deployer and authority.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR provides evidence artifacts; institutions provide oversight and determinations.
  • NADIR does not adjudicate model correctness, legality, or compliance status.
  • Authorization, access, and disclosure are defined by deployment policy and governance agreements.
[DISCLOSURE TIERS]

Policy-scoped redaction under verification

Oversight often requires a “verify without exposure” posture. NADIR supports tiered disclosure: high-level public summaries and controlled review views for authorized audiences. Redaction and masking are policy-scoped so integrity checks remain valid even when sensitive fields are withheld.

[PUBLIC / REDACTED]

High-level summary

Non-sensitive metadata and policy identifiers suitable for public description. No proprietary internals. No default exposure of prompts, inputs, outputs, or operational details unless policy explicitly allows.

[CONTROLLED REVIEW]

Authorized verification view

Expanded fields required to validate integrity, provenance, and policy alignment, subject to authorization and agreement. Masking rules can preserve privacy and trade secrets while allowing verification checks.

[CASE-SPECIFIC]

Disclosure by mandate

Where legally required, case-specific disclosure can be performed under formal process. NADIR’s role is to preserve artifact integrity and a verifiable lineage, not to determine disclosure scope.

[MASKING MODEL]

Capture policy defines what is included, what is excluded, and what is masked. Where fields must be withheld, artifacts can preserve verification properties via stable identifiers, hashed representations, or disclosure-tier segmentation, as defined in the Capture Policy & Masking Schema under controlled review.

[VERIFICATION]

Verification sequence

Verification is a deterministic procedure: canonicalize, recompute hash, validate signature, and confirm policy identifiers. Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy. It does not confirm correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance status of the underlying system.

[STEP 01]

Canonicalize

Serialize the artifact deterministically for verification. JSON artifacts may use RFC 8785 canonicalization where specified by the verification contract.

[STEP 02]

Recompute hash

Recompute the digest over the canonical artifact to detect modification. Hash suites are deployment-defined; contracts specify inputs and expected outputs for PASS/FAIL behavior.

[STEP 03]

Validate signature

Validate that the artifact signature matches the computed digest and the declared verification key. Key custody and trust anchors are governed by deployment configuration.

[STEP 04]

Confirm policy alignment

Confirm Policy ID, Policy version, and policy hash (where applicable). Verify that the artifact claims match the policy scope that governed capture and masking.

[STEP 05]

Check timestamps (optional)

Where configured, validate time anchoring attestations (e.g., TSA or equivalent). Timestamp posture depends on deployment architecture and governance requirements.

[OUTPUT]

Produce review record

Produce a verification result suitable for audit trails: PASS/FAIL, reason codes, policy identifiers, and reviewer metadata as allowed by disclosure policy.

[BOUNDARY]
  • Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy; it does not certify correctness or compliance.
  • Disclosure and access are controlled by authorization, mandate, and policy scope.
  • Admissibility and evidentiary weight are determined by relevant authorities.
