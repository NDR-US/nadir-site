Roadmap

The goal is proof before scale: a deterministic artifact pipeline, a verification harness, and a retention-ready chain-of-custody workflow.

Phase 1 — Minimal viable artifact (MVA)

Deliverable: deterministic artifact record + reproducibility tests under defined conditions.

Define capture boundary

Specify what is captured at inference time and what is excluded.

Canonicalize

Normalize ordering, rounding, serialization, and metadata.

DWSH signing

Generate tamper-evident signatures over the abstracted representation.

Verification harness

Prove repeatability; detect tampering and mismatch states.

Phase 2 — Audit workflow & chain-of-custody

Deliverable: retention-ready record format + retrieval + verification procedure.

Record schema

Timestamping, provenance metadata, input hash, abstraction ID, signature, verification fields.

Retention posture

Storage and retention aligned to evidentiary preservation and audit review practices.

Review outputs

Controlled reports for oversight review without disclosing proprietary internals.

Phase 3 — Controlled expansion

Deliverable: broader coverage while maintaining determinism and constrained disclosure.

Model class extension

Define capture + canonicalization per model class and deployment mode.

Environment hardening

Document determinism boundaries across hardware, batching, and distributed inference.

Third-party validation

Verification steps suitable for oversight without trade secret disclosure.

Compliance alignment

Map artifacts to oversight expectations without claiming correctness or interpretability-by-disclosure.

Quality bar: NADIR expands only after determinism and verification are proven in constrained scope. That is the difference between “idea” and “infrastructure.”
