Proof → Verification → Workflow → Expansion

Roadmap

The path from concept to infrastructure is proof. NADIR prioritizes deterministic artifact generation, independent verification, and chain-of-custody workflow design before scope expansion.

Phase 1 — Minimal viable artifact

Deliverable: canonical artifact + integrity sealing + repeatability tests under defined conditions.

Define capture boundary

Specify fields included, excluded, and masked. Document the policy scope and disclosure tiers.

Canonicalize

Normalize ordering, serialization, rounding, and metadata to remove accidental entropy.

Integrity sealing

Hash and sign the canonical artifact; define key custody and verification procedure.

Verification harness

Prove repeatability under identical conditions and detect tampering or mismatch states.

Phase 2 — Chain-of-custody workflow

Deliverable: retention-ready record format + retrieval + verification steps for review boards.

Record schema

Timestamping, provenance, policy ID, disclosure tier, integrity fields, verification fields.

Retention posture

Storage and retention aligned to evidentiary preservation and audit review expectations.

Review output

Controlled reports that preserve confidentiality while enabling verification.

Phase 3 — Controlled expansion

Deliverable: broaden coverage while maintaining determinism boundaries and constrained disclosure.

Model class extension

Define capture/canonicalization per model type and deployment mode (batching, distributed inference).

Environment hardening

Document determinism boundaries across hardware, runtimes, and configuration drift.

Third-party validation protocol

Verification steps suitable for independent oversight without requiring trade secret disclosure.

Compliance mapping

Map artifacts to review expectations without claiming correctness or certification.

Quality Bar
  • NADIR expands only after determinism and verification are proven in constrained scope.
  • Claims remain limited to integrity, provenance, and verification workflow support.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized reviewers.
