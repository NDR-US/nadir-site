Roadmap

The path from concept to infrastructure is proof. NADIR prioritizes canonical artifacts, independent verification, and chain-of-custody workflow design before expanding scope.

Phase 1 — Minimal viable artifact

Deliverable: canonical artifact + integrity sealing + repeatability tests under defined conditions.

Capture boundary

Define included, excluded, and masked fields; publish a policy scope and disclosure tiers.

Canonical schema

Normalize ordering, serialization, and metadata to reduce ambiguity and accidental entropy.

Integrity sealing

Hash and sign artifacts; define key custody and verification procedure.

Verification harness

Prove repeatability under defined conditions; detect tampering and mismatch states.

Phase 2 — Chain-of-custody workflow

Deliverable: retention-ready record format + retrieval + verification steps suitable for review boards.

Record schema

Timestamp, provenance, policy ID, disclosure tier, integrity fields, verification fields.

Retention posture

Storage and retention aligned to evidentiary preservation and audit review expectations.

Review output

Controlled reporting that enables verification without unnecessary disclosure.

Phase 3 — Controlled expansion

Deliverable: broaden coverage while maintaining determinism boundaries and constrained disclosure.

Model & runtime coverage

Define capture/canonicalization by deployment mode (batching, distributed inference, runtime differences).

Third-party validation protocol

Verification steps suitable for independent oversight without trade secret disclosure.

Quality Bar
  • Expand only after repeatability and verification are proven in constrained scope.
  • Claims remain limited to integrity, provenance, and workflow support.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized reviewers.
