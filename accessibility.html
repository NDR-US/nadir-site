Accessibility Statement

NADIR is designed to be usable by a broad range of people, including those using assistive technologies. This statement describes accessibility goals, testing posture, and how to report issues.

Read accessibility posture Report an issue
[STANDARD]

Accessibility target

NADIR aims to align with WCAG 2.2 Level AA principles for public-facing pages and institutional review interfaces, subject to ongoing iteration and system evolution.

[FOUNDATION]

Semantic structure

Pages are structured with readable heading order, consistent navigation landmarks, and clear focus states to support screen readers and keyboard navigation.

[KEYBOARD]

Operable controls

Interactive elements are designed to be reachable and usable without a mouse. Motion behaviors are scrub-linked and avoid attention-grabbing effects.

[MOTION]

Reduced motion

Where supported, NADIR respects “prefers-reduced-motion” and will limit scroll-linked transforms for users who opt out of motion.

[TESTING POSTURE]
  • Manual review: periodic keyboard-only navigation checks, focus visibility review, and heading/landmark structure validation.
  • Assistive technology: targeted checks with common screen reader/browser combinations where feasible.
  • Contrast and readability: typography and color choices prioritize low-chroma authority while maintaining legibility.
[KNOWN LIMITATIONS]

NADIR is under active development. Some pages or interactive elements may change rapidly. If you encounter an accessibility barrier, report it and include the page URL, device/browser, and a brief description.

[CONTACT]

Accessibility issues may be reported to contact@nadir-ai.com. NADIR intends to review reports and respond within a reasonable timeframe appropriate to project stage and resourcing.

[BOUNDARY]
  • This statement describes goals and operating posture; it is not a certification of compliance.
  • Accessibility posture may evolve as pages, tooling, and institutional workflows expand.
  • Institutional deployments may require additional accessibility commitments under separate written agreement.
