Privacy-by-design • Integrity-first • Constrained disclosure

Governance posture

NADIR is designed for oversight contexts. Its governance posture prioritizes privacy-by-design, minimized collection, integrity verification, and disciplined scope boundaries to support accountable deployment without unnecessary disclosure.

Principles

These principles guide how NADIR is built and how it is intended to be deployed.

Privacy-by-design

Capture scope is policy-defined and minimized. Unnecessary personal data collection is explicitly excluded.

Constrained disclosure

Verification workflows are designed to function without exposing proprietary weights, confidential code, or sensitive architecture.

Integrity-first

Artifacts are sealed to support authenticity validation and tamper detection under defined procedures.

Operational realism

Claims remain limited to what can be verified. NADIR does not claim correctness, certification, or safety guarantees.

Policies for the AI era (NADIR scope)

NADIR’s scope is evidentiary infrastructure. The following policy areas describe how the system aligns with institutional expectations.

01 / Data privacy

Minimized collection, explicit exclusions, defined retention posture, and policy-scoped disclosure tiers.

02 / Responsible oversight

Artifacts support review processes by authorized institutions. NADIR does not replace governance or human authorization.

03 / Audit integrity standards

Canonical schemas, hashing, signing, and verification procedures designed for chain-of-custody compatible workflows.

04 / Deployment risk posture

Verification procedures should be tailored to system risk. NADIR supports process rigor but does not define policy outcomes.

05 / Sovereignty and control

Deploying organizations retain custody of their data and keys. NADIR is designed to avoid forced centralization.

06 / Human rights alignment

NADIR is not intended for unlawful surveillance or rights-degrading deployment. Ethical use and legal compliance are required for use.

Boundary Declaration
  • NADIR is not a regulator, certification body, or compliance determination engine.
  • NADIR does not evaluate legality, fairness, or correctness of model decisions.
  • NADIR produces integrity-sealed artifacts to support authorized oversight workflows.
