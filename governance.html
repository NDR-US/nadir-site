GOVERNANCE POSTURE

Governance in this context refers to structural design principles embedded in the integrity layer itself—not external regulatory authority.

01

Minimization by Design

Capture scope is explicitly defined prior to generation. Only declared fields are recorded; unrelated telemetry is excluded by default configuration. Minimization is structural, not aspirational.

02

Controlled Disclosure

Artifacts enable verification of integrity without disclosing proprietary architecture, model weights, or source code. Disclosure tiers are defined before artifact generation.

03

Deterministic Auditability

Verification is mechanical and reproducible across heterogeneous environments. Interpretation remains an institutional function; integrity remains an architectural one.

04

Non-Adjudication

NADIR does not determine legality, fairness, or correctness. It provides integrity-sealed records to the institutions that make those determinations.

Alignment Posture

NADIR’s architecture reflects principles consistent with contemporary integrity frameworks, including structured record-keeping and purpose limitation. NADIR does not claim formal endorsement by regulatory bodies; it provides the infrastructure required to support their mandates.

Governance Position

Autonomous systems increasingly operate in environments where oversight matters. Infrastructure must reflect that reality.
