Governance Posture

Architectural Boundaries

Governance here refers to structural design principles embedded in the integrity layer itself — explicit capture scope, deterministic artifact generation, and controlled disclosure — not external regulatory authority. CEYO produces records. Institutions make determinations.

[01] Minimization by Design

Policy Before Collection

Capture scope is explicitly defined prior to artifact generation. Only fields declared within policy are recorded; unrelated telemetry is excluded by default configuration. Policy identifiers and versions are embedded within artifacts so boundaries remain reviewable.

Structural minimization · Explicit boundaries
[02] Controlled Disclosure

Verification Without Exposure

Oversight may require validation without forced exposure of proprietary weights, confidential internals, or trade-secret implementations. Disclosure tiers are defined before artifact generation. Redaction is policy-defined, not improvised post hoc.

Tiered access · Policy-defined masking
[03] Deterministic Auditability

Reproducible Integrity

Artifacts are canonicalized and sealed deterministically so independent parties can validate integrity across heterogeneous environments. Verification is mechanical. Interpretation remains institutional.

RFC 8785 · Hash + signature · Independent verification
[04] Non-Adjudication

Infrastructure, Not Authority

CEYO does not determine correctness, fairness, legality, compliance, or admissibility. It does not replace institutional review mechanisms, compliance teams, legal advisory processes, or regulatory authority. It provides integrity-sealed records.

Evidence substrate · Institutional oversight
Boundary: References to external frameworks are descriptive only. CEYO does not claim endorsement, certification, or formal alignment by any regulatory body or standards institution.
