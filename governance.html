Governance
By Design

CEYO governance defines how capture policy is authored, approved, versioned, and verified—along with how disclosure tiers, key custody, and independent verification roles are controlled without merging evidence infrastructure with decision authority.

Governance Model

Roles &
Authority

CEYO is structurally decoupled: it produces artifacts, not decisions. Governance assigns authority to operators and institutions, while ensuring verification can be performed without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

Policy Scope authority
Capture policy defines inclusion, exclusion, masking, tiering, and suites. Policy is versioned and referenced by identifier in each artifact.
Custody Key authority
Signing keys remain in client-controlled HSM/KMS/TEE. CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.

Controls

Policy & Change Control

01 · Versioning Immutable refs
Each artifact records policy identifier/version. Changes produce new versions; older artifacts remain verifiable against their original policy references.
02 · Approval Defined authority
Policies require explicit approval before production. Approval artifacts document scope boundaries, tiering, and suite selection as declared governance inputs.
03 · Key Rotation Custodian-led
Rotation events are controlled by the key custodian. Verification uses the corresponding public key material for the artifact’s recorded signing context.
04 · Fail-Open Continuity
Inference continuity remains independent. If sealing fails, inference proceeds; failures are logged and artifacts are marked unsealed for review.

Disclosure Governance

Tiered
Access

Governance defines how artifacts are shared across tiers. Tiering supports integrity verification without default exposure of model weights, proprietary architecture, or confidential internals.

Public Minimal
Public-facing artifacts disclose only what governance allows for non-sensitive contexts; typically redacted and metadata-limited.
Controlled Review
Controlled tiers enable institutional audits with expanded fields under NDA/authority constraints, preserving compartmentalization.

INDEP
Boundary Declaration · V2026.02.19

CEYO produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance outcomes. Operators and institutions retain responsibility for determinations.

Governance defines scope and disclosure. Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope—not correctness of model output.
