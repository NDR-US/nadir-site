[PRINCIPLES]

Governance principles

Governance is implemented through capture policy, disclosure tiers, and verification workflows. Determinations remain with authorized reviewers.

[PRIVACY-BY-DESIGN]

Collect only what is necessary, define scope explicitly, and record policy identifiers and versions to preserve clarity under review.

[CONTROLLED DISCLOSURE]

Enable verification without default exposure of weights, confidential architecture, or proprietary source code through tiered disclosure.

[AUDITABILITY]

Prefer canonical records designed for formal review over ad-hoc logs. Verification steps are designed to be independently repeatable.

[NON-ADJUDICATION]

NADIR produces records. Institutions and authorized operators perform oversight and make determinations under their authority.

[NIST IR 8596]

NADIR’s design posture is informed by the NIST IR 8596 Cyber AI Profile, which addresses cybersecurity considerations specific to AI systems. This reference does not constitute endorsement, certification, or formal alignment by NIST.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, or correctness.
  • NADIR does not guarantee inference reproducibility.
  • NADIR supports integrity and provenance verification under policy.
  • Admissibility is determined by the relevant authority.
