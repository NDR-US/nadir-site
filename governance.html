PRIVACY-BY-DESIGN · CONTROLLED DISCLOSURE · NON-ADJUDICATION

Governance

NADIR supports accountable review while minimizing collection and preserving confidentiality boundaries.

Principles

Privacy-by-design

Collect only what is necessary, define scope explicitly, and record policy identifiers and versions.

Controlled disclosure

Enable verification without default exposure of weights, confidential architecture, or proprietary source code.

Auditability

Prefer canonical records designed for formal review over ad-hoc logs.

Non-adjudication

NADIR produces records. Institutions make determinations.

NIST IR 8596

NADIR’s design posture is informed by the NIST IR 8596 Cyber AI Profile, which addresses cybersecurity considerations specific to AI systems. NADIR’s artifact layer is designed to support integrity-oriented and oversight-aligned objectives consistent with that profile. This reference does not constitute endorsement or certification by NIST.

Boundary declaration
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, or correctness.
  • NADIR does not guarantee inference reproducibility.
  • NADIR does not assume operational control over inference infrastructure.
  • NADIR supports integrity and provenance verification under policy.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized reviewers.
  • Admissibility of artifacts in any legal or administrative proceeding is determined by the relevant authority.
