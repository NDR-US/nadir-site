Company · Neutrality · Independent verification

Built for formal review.

CEYO produces deterministic, integrity-sealed audit artifacts at the inference boundary. Verification is mechanical: canonicalize → hash → signature → policy alignment. CEYO produces records; institutions make determinations.

The posture

CEYO closes the gap between “telemetry” and “evidence” by generating canonical artifacts that can be verified later without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

The boundary

CEYO does not certify compliance, legality, or decision correctness. It does not guarantee inference reproducibility. It provides integrity-sealed records to support oversight processes.

Foundations
  • Scope first: capture is policy-defined and versioned.
  • Seal second: canonicalize deterministically before hashing.
  • Verify last: independent recomputation yields PASS/FAIL outcomes.

Principles

Constraints that keep the system credible under scrutiny.

Neutrality

Evidence infrastructure stays separate from decision authority.

Verifiability

Determinism + sealing enable independent recomputation and verification.

Minimal disclosure

Tiered disclosure supports review without default exposure of internals.

Operator control

Non-custodial keys: signing authority remains with operators (HSM/KMS/TEE).

Evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority. That separation preserves neutrality and avoids structural conflicts between record generation and policy enforcement.
