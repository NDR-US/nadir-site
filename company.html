Company · Purpose · Boundaries

CEYO builds evidentiary infrastructure for AI inference.

CEYO exists to close an oversight gap: most AI deployments generate telemetry, but not audit-ready evidence that can survive formal review.

CEYO is designed as a neutral audit layer for AI inference systems. It attaches at the inference boundary and produces deterministic, cryptographically sealed artifacts that represent policy-scoped inference-time context. Those artifacts are built to support integrity verification and forensic reconstruction workflows performed by authorized reviewers.

The core principle is structural: evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority. CEYO does not make decisions, certify outcomes, or determine compliance. It produces records. Institutions and operators retain responsibility for determinations.

What CEYO is

CEYO is record infrastructure. In deployment, operators define an explicit capture policy that governs what is included, excluded, and masked. Captured fields are canonicalized deterministically (RFC 8785) and sealed using cryptographic hashing and digital signatures under a non-custodial key model. Verification is performed by recomputing canonicalization and hashes, validating signatures, and confirming policy alignment.

What CEYO is not

CEYO is not an AI platform, a safety certification body, a regulatory authority, or a compliance determination engine. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or policy compliance of an AI system. It does not guarantee inference reproducibility. It does not guarantee that artifacts will satisfy the evidentiary sufficiency standard of any specific proceeding.

How CEYO is used

CEYO is designed for environments where the cost of ambiguity is unacceptable: regulated decisions, safety-critical autonomy, incident reconstruction, and institutional accountability workflows. Deployment options include sidecar, gateway, or wrapper patterns, selected by the operator based on architecture and latency tolerance. Fail-open posture is supported so inference continuity remains independent.

Commitment to lawful use

CEYO’s intended posture is to strengthen accountability, not circumvent it. Operators remain responsible for lawful deployment, appropriate capture scope, human authorization controls for consequential decisions, and chain-of-custody integrity outside the sealing boundary. Prohibited deployments include attempts to obscure oversight, automate consequential decisions without independent human authorization controls, or target individuals based on protected characteristics without lawful basis.
