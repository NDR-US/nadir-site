Architecture

NADIR follows a strict mechanics chain: attach at the inference boundary, generate a canonical artifact schema, seal it with integrity controls, enforce controlled disclosure, and enable independent verification.

Operational integration Document suite

STAGE 1 — CAPTURE MODEL

NADIR is designed to attach non-intrusively at the inference boundary (sidecar, gateway, or wrapper). Capture scope is policy-defined and explicitly bounded to avoid unnecessary collection and to preserve confidential implementation boundaries.

Inference boundary attachment
Instrumentation point is outside core model logic to reduce adoption friction and avoid mutating weights.
Policy-scoped capture
Capture includes defined inputs and runtime context; excludes proprietary weights, architecture details, and unrelated data.
Mandatory fail-open architecture
If the sealing layer encounters a failure condition, NADIR does not interrupt inference. The primary system continues operating. A fail-open exception is logged and the unsealed event is flagged for review. System availability is never compromised by the artifact layer.

STAGE 2 — ARTIFACT SCHEMA

Captured context is normalized into a canonical artifact structure intended to support integrity verification under defined capture conditions. The schema is designed for audit workflows and chain-of-custody compatible storage.

Canonical artifact fields (example)

Timestamp · Provenance metadata · Input hash · Policy ID · Policy version · Policy hash · Capture environment fingerprint · Canonicalization version · Disclosure tier · Integrity fields · Verification fields

STAGE 3 — INTEGRITY MODEL

Artifacts are sealed using cryptographic hashing and digital signatures to support authenticity validation and tamper detection. Default reference implementations may include SHA-256 for hashing and ECDSA (e.g., P-256) for digital signatures. Canonicalization conforms to RFC 8785 (JSON Canonicalization Scheme) — the sole normative serialization method applied to artifact content prior to hashing. Deployment-specific cryptographic suites may vary based on regulatory or operational requirements.

Hashing

Produces a fixed digest of the canonical artifact to detect modification.

Digital signature

Authenticates artifact origin under key custody and verification policies.

Time anchoring
Supports evidentiary posture by binding artifact integrity to trusted timestamp authorities (TSA), hardware security module–based time sources, or cryptographically verifiable time attestation mechanisms as defined in the deployment configuration.

STAGE 4 — DISCLOSURE FIELD MODEL

NADIR supports tiered visibility: reviewers verify integrity and provenance without receiving proprietary internals. Disclosure boundaries are policy-defined and can vary by audience and agreement.

Public / redacted

High-level description of artifacts and controls without sensitive operational detail.

Controlled review

Expanded fields for authorized reviewers while preserving trade secret boundaries.

STAGE 5 — VERIFICATION WORKFLOW

Verification is performed by recomputing hashes over the canonical artifact, validating signatures, and confirming policy alignment. Verification confirms artifact integrity and provenance — not inference reproducibility or decision correctness. Verification failure results in a formally reportable integrity exception under the defined review workflow.

BOUNDARY DECLARATION
  • NADIR verifies artifact integrity and provenance under policy.
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, or model correctness.
  • NADIR does not guarantee inference reproducibility.
  • NADIR does not assume operational control over inference infrastructure.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized institutions and operators.
