Human Rights & Lawful Use Policy

1. Purpose

This policy establishes the human rights and lawful use boundaries governing deployment of CEYO evidentiary infrastructure. CEYO is record infrastructure. It does not adjudicate decisions, certify compliance, or determine lawfulness. This policy defines the intended design posture of CEYO infrastructure and the obligations of operators who deploy it.

2. Foundational commitments

CEYO is designed to support accountability, not circumvent it. Deployment is intended to strengthen oversight processes, not replace, delay, or obscure them.

  • Oversight primacy: deployments that materially restrict legitimate review are outside intended use.
  • Human authorization: CEYO does not make decisions. Independent human authorization controls must exist for consequential outcomes.
  • Proportionality: capture scope must be proportionate to the defined oversight purpose.
  • Non-discrimination: CEYO must not be deployed to systematically target protected groups without lawful basis.

3. Lawful use requirements

Operators deploying CEYO represent and warrant that:

  • Deployment is authorized under applicable law and institutional policy.
  • Use of CEYO artifacts complies with applicable domestic law and, where relevant, international humanitarian law.
  • Capture scope has been reviewed for compliance with applicable privacy and data protection requirements.
  • Independent human authorization controls exist for any deployment involving consequential decisions.

CEYO does not independently verify operator compliance with these representations. If an operator’s representations are materially false or deployment is found inconsistent with this policy, CEYO may terminate access or decline engagement pursuant to Section 6.

4. Defense & national security deployments

CEYO may be deployed in defense and national security contexts for integrity verification and forensic reconstruction workflows. Such deployments are subject to additional boundaries:

  • Artifacts are intended to support oversight boards, accountability procedures, and institutional review workflows.
  • CEYO does not replace operational command authority or legal review functions.
  • Operators remain responsible for compliance with applicable rules of engagement, international humanitarian law where relevant, DoD Directive 3000.09 (or equivalent), and export controls including ITAR/EAR where applicable.
  • CEYO does not certify that any decision, action, or outcome was lawful, proportionate, or compliant.
  • CEYO does not guarantee that artifacts satisfy evidentiary sufficiency standards; that determination rests with the relevant authority.

5. Prohibited deployments

CEYO must not be deployed to:

  • Circumvent, obscure, or materially impede legitimate oversight processes.
  • Automate consequential decisions without independent human authorization controls.
  • Target individuals based on protected characteristics without lawful basis.
  • Support deployments prohibited under applicable export control law.
  • Generate artifacts intended to misrepresent decision context or provenance.

6. Operator obligations

Operators assume full responsibility for ensuring deployment is lawful, authorized, and consistent with this policy. CEYO does not monitor deployments in real time. Material violations may constitute grounds for termination of access or refusal of continued services under applicable agreement terms.

7. Escalation & review

Concerns regarding deployment consistency with this policy may be directed to: contact@ceyo.ai

CEYO reserves the right to decline or terminate engagements inconsistent with this policy at its sole discretion.

8. Boundary declaration

CEYO produces integrity-sealed records to support oversight processes. CEYO does not certify compliance, determine legality, validate decision correctness, guarantee inference reproducibility, certify that artifacts satisfy any specific legal or regulatory proceeding, or substitute for institutional review.

This policy does not constitute legal advice. Operators should consult appropriate counsel regarding their deployment context.

9. Version control

This policy is versioned and subject to revision. The current version identifier appears in the document header. Operators are responsible for confirming reference to the current version prior to deployment.
