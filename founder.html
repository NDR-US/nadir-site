[LETTER]

Evidence first, judgment later

NADIR is record infrastructure. Institutions make determinations.

AI inference systems are increasingly deployed in environments where ambiguity is expensive. In many deployments, outputs and surface telemetry exist, but the records are not designed to survive adversarial scrutiny. What is logged may be helpful operationally, yet insufficient when a decision is disputed later.

NADIR exists to close that gap with a disciplined approach: policy-scoped capture, deterministic canonicalization, cryptographic integrity sealing, and controlled disclosure. The objective is to support review processes without forcing builders to expose model weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation details.

The boundary is deliberate. NADIR does not claim to determine whether an output was correct, fair, lawful, or compliant. NADIR produces verifiable records that allow authorized institutions and operators to perform oversight using their own standards, processes, and authority.

The technical posture is designed to be robust across environments. For JSON artifacts, NADIR uses deterministic canonicalization (RFC 8785) prior to hashing so that integrity checks remain stable across heterogeneous systems. Integrity sealing uses deployment-defined cryptographic suites. Key custody is non-custodial by design: signing keys are generated and retained within the client’s controlled environment (HSM/KMS), and NADIR does not possess private keys.

NADIR is being built with institutional workflows in mind: constrained disclosure, chain-of-custody posture, and verification steps that can be performed independently. Where the implementation is still under active development, the roadmap states the scope and maturity explicitly.

— Brian Covarrubias

[CORE POSITION] NADIR is record infrastructure, not adjudication.
[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, or model correctness.
  • NADIR verifies artifact integrity and provenance under defined policy.
  • Admissibility and oversight conclusions are determined by relevant authorities and authorized reviewers.
