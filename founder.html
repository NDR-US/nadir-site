Why I Built
NADIR

I’m Brian Covarrubias. For the past decade, I have lived at the seam between production-scale machine-learning systems and the auditors asked to explain them.

I have testified in breach-response panels, written the integrity sections of two Fortune 100 compliance programs, and served on an IEEE working group defining provenance for autonomous pipelines. Every investigation reveals the same gap: logs were never designed to survive formal review.

The problem I kept seeing

1. Telemetry ≠ Evidence

Application logs record events for debugging, not as a canonical, policy-scoped artifact a regulator or court will accept as immutable proof.

2. Proprietary Paradox

Operators must prove system integrity without exposing proprietary weights, sensitive prompts, or confidential internal architecture.

3. Liability Drift

When anomalies occur, the operator, vendor, and host blame one another because no common, cryptographic integrity layer exists to anchor the truth.

The Digital Notary

NADIR operates beside an inference engine—never inside it—and emits a sealed artifact for every model run.

  • ● Deterministic: RFC 8785 canonicalization
  • ● Tamper-evident: SHA-256 / ECDSA P-256
  • ● Weight-neutral: No IP leaves the environment
  • ● Non-custodial: Keys remain in your HSM/KMS

"Think of it as a digital notary for AI inference. It never judges a decision; it proves the record is authentic and complete."

Why Now

Regulations have caught up. The EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF both require systematic, tamper-resistant logging for high-risk systems, yet today’s stacks still treat logging as a developer convenience. NADIR closes that gap without demanding that innovators surrender their IP.

The Commitment

  • • Minimal collection, maximal verifiability.
  • • Clear boundaries: Evidence vs. Judgment.
  • • Public, versioned specifications (V2026.02) for math-audit.

If you run or regulate AI that cannot afford ambiguity, I’m ready to show you a live demo.
