Founder’s letter

NADIR is being developed as evidentiary infrastructure—focused on integrity, verification, and constrained disclosure—so that oversight can be performed without forcing exposure of proprietary internals or compromising legitimate operational security.

Letter

As AI systems move into higher-consequence environments, the institutions responsible for oversight need more than outputs and dashboards. They need verifiable records—bounded by policy, preserved with integrity controls, and structured for later review.

NADIR is designed to address that gap. It is not an authority and it does not certify compliance or correctness. It is infrastructure that helps organizations and reviewers answer a narrower, legally defensible question: whether an artifact is authentic, unmodified, and consistent with a defined capture policy.

The aim is practical: support reconstruction and verification workflows while preserving confidentiality, trade secrets, and legitimate security boundaries. The future of accountability will require systems that can be reviewed without forcing disclosure that undermines innovation or operational safety.

NADIR will remain disciplined in scope. Claims will remain limited to integrity, provenance, and verification workflow support. Oversight conclusions belong to authorized institutions and operators—not to this system.

Brian Covarrubias
Founder, NADIR
San Diego, California, United States
