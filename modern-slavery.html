Modern Slavery & Human Rights Statement

This statement describes NADIR’s expectations and operating posture regarding modern slavery, human trafficking, and human rights risks across our operations and supply chain. It is designed for institutional procurement review and will evolve as NADIR scales.

Enter statement Report a concern
[SCOPE]

What this covers

This statement applies to NADIR’s internal operations and to suppliers and service providers who support NADIR’s business functions. It is not a representation of third-party compliance, nor a certification.

[COMMITMENT]

Human rights posture

NADIR intends to operate with respect for human rights and to avoid supporting practices inconsistent with modern slavery and human trafficking prohibitions.

[RISK]

Risk awareness

NADIR prioritizes risk-based review of suppliers based on geography, labor intensity, and service type, with heightened attention to vendors involved in physical goods or labor-intensive services.

[EXPECTATIONS]

Supplier standards

Where feasible, NADIR seeks to work with suppliers and providers that maintain appropriate labor and human rights policies, including prohibition of forced labor and child labor.

[DUE DILIGENCE]
  • Supplier onboarding review: NADIR may request basic corporate identity information, ownership disclosures where appropriate, and confirmation of labor policy posture for higher-risk services.
  • Contract posture: where commercially reasonable, supplier agreements may include obligations to comply with applicable labor laws and to prohibit forced labor and trafficking.
  • Escalation: credible allegations may trigger internal review, requests for remediation, or discontinuation of the relationship depending on severity and feasibility.
[REPORTING]

Concerns related to unethical labor practices may be reported to contact@nadir-ai.com. Reports should include enough detail to permit review (vendor name, relationship context, and supporting information where available).

[BOUNDARY]
  • This statement describes intent and operating posture; it is not legal advice.
  • NADIR does not represent supplier compliance, certification status, or audit outcomes absent a separate written agreement.
  • Statement updates are tracked by version; institutional reviewers should reference the version tag shown on this page.
