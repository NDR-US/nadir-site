FAQ.

Addressing Architectural Skepticism

How does NADIR differ from standard observability logging?

Standard logging is interpretive and mutable. NADIR is forensic. By applying RFC 8785 canonicalization and cryptographic sealing at the moment of inference, we produce an immutable artifact that cannot be altered retroactively. We do not monitor performance; we prove intent.

Does NADIR introduce significant latency to the model?

No. The NADIR sidecar is designed for high-concurrency environments. Performance benchmarks confirm a latency overhead of <1.5%. In "fail-open" configurations, the inference service proceeds regardless of the integrity layer's status to ensure zero downtime.

Can NADIR "fix" biased or incorrect AI outputs?

No. Our posture is one of Non-Adjudication. NADIR does not intervene in model logic or correct outputs. Our ethical duty is to provide an honest, unalterable record so that human institutions can perform the necessary adjudication.

What happens if we lose our private keys?

Because NADIR is non-custodial, we do not store or manage your private keys. Loss of keys results in the permanent inability to verify artifacts or unlock tiered content. We recommend integration with institutional HSMs.
