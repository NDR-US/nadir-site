Trust Center

NADIR’s trust posture is defined by explicit boundaries: policy-scoped capture, constrained disclosure, integrity-bound records, and non-custodial key architecture. This page summarizes the institutional baseline.

[DATA]

Data boundaries and sovereignty

NADIR is designed so artifacts remain in the operator-controlled environment. Access is granted to authorized institutions under defined scope and disclosure rules.

[MINIMIZATION]

Policy-scoped capture

NADIR records only the metadata and decision-state context defined by policy scope, with explicit inclusion and exclusion.

[RESIDENCY]

Client-controlled retention

Artifacts are retained under the operator’s custody model. NADIR does not require default centralization of sealed records.

[LIMITATION]

Purpose limitation

Audit artifacts are designed for oversight and forensic verification, not for secondary surveillance or model training.

[KEY CUSTODY]

NADIR is designed for non-custodial key management. Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment (HSM/KMS where available). NADIR does not possess or transmit private keys by default.

[INTEGRITY]
  • Canonicalize deterministically prior to hashing (RFC 8785 for JSON artifacts) to ensure cross-environment determinism.
  • Seal artifacts cryptographically for tamper-evidence and later verification.
  • Resource isolation principles (e.g., cgroup-based quotas) support operational non-interference by design intent.
