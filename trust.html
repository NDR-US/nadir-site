[REVIEW POSTURE]

What institutional reviewers need

Trust is built through bounded claims, stable documentation, and verifiable mechanics. NADIR’s posture centers on integrity, provenance, and constrained disclosure: a record can be verified without forcing exposure of proprietary internals by default.

[BOUNDARIES]

Scope is explicit

Capture is policy-scoped: what is included, excluded, and masked is declared. Artifacts can carry policy identifiers, versions, and hashes to support traceability and change control.

[VERIFICATION]

Integrity is checkable

Verification recomputes hashes over the canonical artifact, validates signatures, and checks policy alignment under defined capture conditions. Verification confirms integrity and provenance, not correctness.

[DISCLOSURE]

Tiered visibility

Disclosure tiers allow reviewers to validate authenticity without receiving unnecessary sensitive fields. Expanded fields are made available only to authorized audiences under defined agreements.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR provides record infrastructure; institutions and operators make determinations.
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, correctness, or evidentiary admissibility.
  • Any claims about deployment posture must be evaluated for the specific environment and configuration.
[SECURITY POSTURE]

Security and integrity posture

NADIR is designed to reduce tampering risk for records by sealing artifacts cryptographically and separating record generation from inference logic. Security implementations and control coverage are deployment-specific and may be provided under controlled review.

[CANONICALIZATION]

Deterministic serialization

For JSON artifacts, canonicalization may be applied (e.g., RFC 8785 where specified) prior to hashing to ensure consistent integrity checks across heterogeneous environments.

[SEALING]

Hashing and signatures

Artifacts are sealed via cryptographic hashing and digital signatures. Cryptographic suites are deployment-defined and may vary by regulatory or operational requirements and key custody policy.

[AVAILABILITY]

Fail-open posture

In fail-open mode, inference continues if sealing fails, while the failure is logged and the event is flagged as unsealed. Availability decisions remain deployment-specific and may be policy-configurable.

[SIDE-CAR ISOLATION]

Resource separation

Where deployed as a sidecar, resource isolation may be enforced via containerization and quota controls (e.g., cgroups) to reduce operational back-pressure risk on the inference pathway.

[CRYPTO AGILITY]

Forward readiness

A cryptographic agility posture supports future suite transitions when required. A PQC readiness roadmap may be provided under controlled review for institutional evaluation.

[KEY CUSTODY]

Non-custodial architecture posture

Key custody is central to trust. Institutional reviewers require clarity on who can sign, revoke, rotate, and attest to artifacts. NADIR’s intended posture separates artifact generation from private key custody.

[DECLARATION]

NADIR is designed to support a non-custodial architecture posture. Signing keys are generated and retained within the client’s controlled environment (e.g., HSM/KMS) according to client policy. NADIR does not need to possess or transmit private keys to support verification workflows. Deployment implementation details are provided under controlled review where required.

[DISCLOSURE]
  • Public pages are descriptive only and may remain intentionally high-level.
  • Operational specifications, schemas, and workflow materials can be shared under controlled review.
  • Disclosure tiers are policy-defined and intended to preserve trade secret boundaries and protect sensitive fields.
[DATA SOVEREIGNTY]

Data residency posture

Institutional buyers evaluate where sensitive records live, who can access them, and whether they leave the client environment. Data residency and access control are deployment decisions.

[DEPLOYER CONTROL]

Client-governed storage

Artifact retention posture, storage location, and access roles are defined by the deploying organization to meet jurisdictional requirements and institutional policy.

[ACCESS LOGGING]

Review traceability

Chain-of-custody posture includes retrieval logging and review workflow traceability. Specific retention and access controls are deployment-defined and may be documented under controlled review.

[MINIMIZATION]

Policy-scoped capture

Capture scope is policy-defined and bounded. Where required, masking and redaction rules can be applied to reduce collection of sensitive fields while maintaining integrity verification for the artifact boundary.
