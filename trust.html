[01] Integrity Architecture

Integrity Architecture

CEYO's integrity posture is anchored in determinism, non-custodial key management, and transparent boundary declarations. Every trust claim is reducible to a verifiable procedure.

Canonicalization
RFC 8785 Determinism
JSON artifacts canonicalized before hashing. Identical byte representations across heterogeneous environments. Verification requires no CEYO infrastructure.
Sealing
Hash + Digital Signature
SHA-256 hashing (reference). ECDSA P-256 signatures (reference). Suite configurable per regulatory context. Tamper-evidence and origin authenticity established independently.
Availability
Fail-Open by Default
Sealing failures log and flag without interrupting inference unless operator configures stricter posture. Operational control remains with the deploying institution.
[02] Key Custody Declaration

Non-Custodial Key Architecture

Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment. CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys. This non-custodial architecture preserves a clear separation between infrastructure provider and cryptographic authority.

Supported custody environments
Hardware Security Modules (HSM)
Key Management Services (KMS)
Trusted Execution Environments (TEE)
Separation consequence

Because CEYO does not hold signing keys, artifact authenticity remains anchored to the operator-controlled environment. CEYO infrastructure cannot independently alter or forge signatures.

Independent verifiers may validate artifact signatures against public keys published by the operator. Signature validation confirms the artifact was sealed within the operator's controlled environment and has not been altered since.

[03] Legal Admissibility Primer

Admissibility Primer

This information describes how integrity-sealed artifacts may support admissibility arguments under applicable evidentiary standards. It is informational only and does not constitute legal advice. Admissibility is determined by the relevant authority.

Not legal advice. Operators and reviewers should consult appropriate counsel.

Authenticity
Cryptographic Chain
Digital signatures and deterministic canonicalization support validation that an artifact has not been altered since sealing.
Timeliness
Inference-Time Generation
Artifacts generated at inference-time under defined policy. Optional RFC 3161 TSA or HSM-based time anchoring may support temporal claims.
Systemic Process
Versioned Mechanisms
Policy IDs, version identifiers, and canonicalization version fields document generation as systematic and consistent rather than ad hoc.
Evidentiary Context
FRE 803(6) Reference
Systematic, integrity-protected records may be considered under business records exceptions in certain jurisdictions. Determinations remain with the court or relevant authority.
