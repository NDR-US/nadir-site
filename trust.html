Verification
You Can Recompute

Trust in CEYO is mechanical: canonicalization produces stable bytes; hashing and signatures produce verifiable integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. Oversight can independently recompute and validate.

Integrity proves tamper-evidence and origin authenticity under policy scope.
It does not prove output correctness.
01 / Determinism Stable bytes
Determinism eliminates interpretive log variance. Canonical bytes are the basis for reproducible hashing and signature checks across environments.
02 / Sealing Hash + signature
Hashing provides tamper-evidence; signatures provide provenance (who sealed it). Suites are configurable by operational and regulatory requirement.
03 / Keys Non-custodial
Private keys remain client-controlled in HSM/KMS/TEE. CEYO does not store, transmit, or possess signing keys—cryptographic authority stays institutional.
04 / Disclosure Tiered review
Public / Redacted / Controlled Review tiers are policy-defined pre-generation. Oversight can validate integrity without default exposure of proprietary internals.
VERIFICATION STEPS

Independent Validation
Workflow

Step 1
Obtain artifact + policy
Oversight receives the artifact and the associated policy identifier/version that defines scope and disclosure rules for the record.
Step 2
Canonicalize
Canonicalization produces deterministic bytes. Any verifier can recompute the canonical bytes from the disclosed artifact fields.
Step 3
Recompute hash
The verifier recomputes the digest over canonical bytes. Any mismatch indicates tampering or policy/serialization mismatch.
Step 4
Validate signature
Using the appropriate public key/certificate chain, the verifier validates the signature to confirm provenance under the chosen cryptographic suite.
Step 5
Evaluate scope
Oversight checks the policy boundary: what was included, excluded, masked, and tiered. This determines interpretive confidence, not correctness.
Step 6
Institutional determination
With integrity and scope established, institutions conduct review processes (audit, incident review, legal evaluation) according to their standards.

THREAT MODEL (PUBLIC SUMMARY)

CEYO is designed to reduce the credibility gap created by partial logs and interpretive reconstructions. It targets tampering, ambiguity, and unverifiable provenance by making artifact generation deterministic and sealing cryptographic.

CEYO does not attempt to prove model correctness or replace governance programs. It supports integrity proofs and scope visibility under constrained disclosure.
