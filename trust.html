Boundary: verification is mechanical
Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness, legality, fairness, compliance outcomes, or inference reproducibility.
Integrity Controls

Trust Is Architecture

Trust is not a statement. It is a repeatable procedure that independent parties can execute using deterministic steps and authorized public-key material.

CEYO controls are designed to verify integrity without forcing exposure of proprietary internals beyond declared policy scope.

01 / Determinism Same bytes
Under the same policy version, logically equivalent artifacts produce identical canonical bytes and identical digests across environments.
02 / Canonicalization RFC 8785
RFC 8785 defines deterministic JSON canonicalization rules (ordering, numeric normalization, encoding) to make hashing reproducible.
03 / Sealing Hash + signature
Hashing establishes tamper-evidence. Digital signatures establish origin authenticity under declared public key material.
04 / Key custody Non-custodial
Private signing keys remain within operator-controlled environments (HSM/KMS/TEE). CEYO does not store, transmit, or possess private keys.
Independent Verification

A Deterministic Workflow

Verification is designed to be executed by independent parties using deterministic procedures. It requires no access to model weights or proprietary internals by default.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope, and produces formal outcomes suitable for review workflows.

Verification outcomes
PASS / FAIL / POLICY MISMATCH
Outcome
Integrity Confirmed
Digest matches and signature validates under authorized public key material.
Outcome
Integrity Failed
Hash mismatch or signature failure indicates alteration or invalid origin attribution.
Outcome
Policy Scope Mismatch
Artifact policy identifier/version does not match expected configuration for the review context.
Step 01 Canonicalize
Deterministically canonicalize the artifact using the documented canonicalization procedure (RFC 8785). Canonicalization must match the recorded version.
Step 02 Recompute hash
Recompute the digest over the canonical bytes using the documented algorithm suite and compare to the recorded digest.
Step 03 Validate signature
Validate the digital signature against authorized public key material (or key reference) under the declared key custody posture.
Step 04 Confirm policy
Confirm policy ID and policy version alignment. Where configured, validate policy hash against the referenced policy document under independent custody.
Constrained Disclosure

Verification Without Forced Exposure

Oversight may require validation without exposure of proprietary architecture, confidential internals, or trade-secret implementation.

CEYO artifacts support tiered disclosure so integrity can be validated within declared scope boundaries.

Tier 01 Public / Redacted
Limited metadata and integrity fields suitable for transparency reporting. Proprietary operational detail remains excluded by design.
Tier 02 Controlled Review
Expanded fields for authorized reviewers under agreement, aligned to oversight requirements while preserving trade-secret boundaries.
Tier 03 Full Disclosure
Available only under specific legal or institutional order and subject to applicable agreements. Scope is defined per engagement.
Trust Boundaries

What CEYO Does Not Claim

Trust posture is defined by verifiable mechanics and explicit limitations. CEYO does not expand those boundaries through implication.

The sufficiency of any artifact for legal or regulatory proceedings is determined by the relevant authority, not by CEYO.

Non-claims
CEYO does not certify compliance, admissibility, fairness, legality, or correctness. It does not guarantee inference reproducibility. It provides integrity-sealed records under declared scope.
Transparency SBOM posture
Reference builds maintain SBOM materials (SPDX/CycloneDX) shared under controlled review to support institutional supply chain governance.
Security Disclosure channel
A structured Vulnerability Disclosure Program supports good-faith reporting. Production deployments may be governed by separate agreements.
Civil liberties Minimization
Policy-scoped capture enforces minimization and purpose limitation structurally. Fields outside policy scope are not captured by default configuration.
