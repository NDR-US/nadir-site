Trust Infrastructure · Deterministic mechanics · V2026.02.19

Trust is verifiable.

Trust in evidentiary systems is not established through statements. It is established through reproducible mechanics. CEYO’s trust posture is anchored in determinism, non-custodial cryptography, and explicit boundaries.

Integrity Architecture

Deterministic, sealed, reviewable.

  • Deterministic JSON canonicalization (RFC 8785)
  • Cryptographic hashing over canonical byte representation
  • Digital signatures under deployment-defined suite
  • Policy identifier + version embedded in every artifact
  • Explicit disclosure tier recorded at generation time
Boundary

Trust refers to integrity—not correctness.

Verification confirms whether an artifact has been altered since sealing and whether its provenance aligns with the declared policy scope. Verification does not confirm semantic equivalence, output correctness, legal sufficiency, or compliance.

Non-custodial key architecture

Cryptographic authority remains institutional.

Signing keys are generated and retained within the operator-controlled environment (HSM, KMS, or TEE). CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys. Authenticity remains anchored to the deploying institution.

Deterministic verification

Executable without CEYO infrastructure.

  • Canonicalize under RFC 8785
  • Recompute hash
  • Validate signature against operator public key
  • Confirm Policy ID + version alignment
Availability Discipline

Integrity should not halt operations by default.

Fail-open

Continuity preserved

Inference may continue uninterrupted if sealing fails (unless the operator configures a stricter posture).

Reviewable

Failures are explicit

Seal failure events are logged and artifacts may be marked as unsealed for later institutional review.

Institutional control

Operator decides

Availability is an institutional decision. CEYO provides mechanisms, not authority.

Supply Chain Transparency

SBOM + disclosure discipline.

CEYO maintains a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) for reference builds (e.g., SPDX, CycloneDX) and can share it under controlled review. Transparency is balanced against proprietary boundaries by tiered disclosure.

Security reporting

Vulnerability Disclosure Program

Responsible reporting is supported through a structured VDP for CEYO-controlled properties and reference components. Production deployments are governed by separate contractual security provisions.

Trust Boundaries

Explicit limitations are part of credibility.

  • Does not guarantee immunity from attack
  • Does not certify regulatory compliance
  • Does not guarantee admissibility
  • Does not validate correctness of inference outcomes
