Sustainability disclosure

NADIR’s sustainability posture focuses on compute-to-integrity efficiency: how much additional computational overhead is introduced to achieve verifiable record integrity. This page describes measurement boundaries and disclosure discipline. It is not an environmental certification.

View methodology Trust center
[MEASUREMENT]

Compute-to-integrity ratio

The compute-to-integrity ratio describes the marginal compute introduced by artifact sealing relative to the primary inference workload. It is deployment-specific and must be measured in context.

[HASHING]

Digest overhead

CPU cost associated with canonicalization and hashing of the artifact boundary.

[SIGNING]

Signature operations

Cryptographic signing cost per artifact, dependent on suite selection and key management posture.

[STORAGE]

Retention footprint

Storage overhead introduced by artifact retention policies and disclosure tiers.

[DISCIPLINE]

Measurements must be performed in the target deployment environment. Public values are illustrative only and should not be interpreted as universal performance claims.

[BOUNDARY]

Explicit exclusions

Sustainability statements remain bounded. NADIR does not make broad environmental claims beyond its compute-to-integrity scope.

  • No lifecycle carbon accounting claims are made on this page.
  • No datacenter renewable energy claims are asserted unless independently verified.
  • No offset or neutrality claims are made.
  • Client infrastructure emissions remain outside NADIR’s direct control.
[ARCHITECTURE]

Architectural efficiency

NADIR is designed as a sidecar artifact layer to avoid duplicating inference workloads. Efficiency derives from separation of concerns.

[SEPARATION]

Artifact sealing operates independently of model training and inference pathways.

[FAIL-OPEN]

In fail-open mode, inference continues even if sealing fails, preserving availability while logging integrity exceptions.

Sustainability posture depends on deployment architecture. For institutional review, deployment-specific efficiency measurements may be shared under controlled review.
