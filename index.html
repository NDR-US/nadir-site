Evidentiary
Infrastructure
For AI Inference Systems

NADIR is an attachable integrity and forensic compliance layer that generates policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context.

Artifacts are sealed with integrity controls to support later verification and structured oversight under constrained disclosure. NADIR produces verifiable records; it does not adjudicate model correctness.

Enter Framework Document Suite

Structural deficits in accountability infrastructure.

Most AI deployments retain outputs and surface telemetry but do not preserve canonical, integrity-bound artifacts suitable for formal review. This gap hampers institutional accountability when decisions are contested.

01 // TRACEABILITY

Telemetry ≠ Evidence

Surface logs can describe events but rarely conform to deterministic, integrity-sealed formats.

02 // EXPOSURE

Constrained Review Unsolved

Institutions need verifiability without exposing proprietary weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation details.

03 // RISK

Liability Ambiguity

Without integrity-bound artifacts, disputes rely on partial telemetry and non-reproducible context.

[ Architecture ]

Record infrastructure, not adjudication.

NADIR attaches beside an inference system and produces artifacts structured for verification. Capture scope is policy-defined: what is included, excluded, and masked.

DATA_SCOPE

Policy-scoped capture

NORMALIZE

Canonical artifact schema

Inputs and runtime context are normalized into a schema aligned to audit workflows.

CRYPTOGRAPHY

Integrity sealing

Deterministic canonicalization → hashing → digital signature. Deployment chooses crypto suite.

Boundary Declaration
  • • NADIR does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or policy compliance of AI systems.
  • • NADIR does not certify regulatory compliance or legal admissibility.
  • • NADIR does not guarantee inference reproducibility.
  • • Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy, not decision correctness.
  • • Oversight and admissibility determinations rest with the relevant authority.
NADIR provides verifiable records; authorized institutions provide oversight.
