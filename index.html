Evidentiary Substrate for Autonomous Systems

NADIR attaches beside inference systems to produce policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. Artifacts are sealed for integrity and structured for institutional verification.

Enter framework Platform architecture
[PROBLEM]

Structural deficits in existing AI logging

Telemetry explains what happened. It often fails to preserve normalized, integrity-bound artifacts suitable for custody handling, deterministic verification, and post-incident reconstruction under institutional review.

[RECONSTRUCTION]

Post-incident reconstruction

Surface logs rarely preserve canonical structures required for deterministic replay and verification workflows.

[CONSTRAINED REVIEW]

Oversight without IP exposure

Institutions require verifiability without default disclosure of weights, confidential architecture, or trade secrets.

[DISPUTE RISK]

Liability ambiguity

Without integrity-bound artifacts, disputes rely on partial telemetry and non-reproducible context.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance.
  • Authorized institutions and operators make determinations.
  • Artifacts are policy-scoped with explicit inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules.
[SOLUTION]

The NADIR sidecar model

NADIR attaches beside inference systems and generates canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time context. The objective is verifiable records under constrained disclosure.

[CAPTURE]

Policy-scoped capture

Define what metadata and decision-state context is included. Exclusions and masking rules are explicit.

[CANONICAL]

Canonical structure

Normalize telemetry into a stable artifact schema designed for later review and verification.

[SEAL]

Integrity sealing

Canonicalize deterministically before hashing (RFC 8785 for JSON artifacts), then hash and sign for tamper-evidence.
