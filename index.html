Bridging the structural gap between raw data and durable proof through decoupled, cryptographically sealed inference artifacts.

// Core_Philosophy

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, current infrastructure produces telemetry that was never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

NADIR exists to address that structural gap. It attaches beside inference systems to produce policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context.

System Parameters:

• NADIR does not evaluate outputs.

• NADIR does not adjudicate compliance.

• NADIR produces records.

The Accountability
Gap.

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is questioned—via regulatory inquiry, audit, or litigation—organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.
