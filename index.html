[Structural deficit]

The Structural Deficit

Modern AI systems generate logs, telemetry, and traces. These signals describe activity, but they do not constitute evidence.

Telemetry may show that a model executed It may show input and output It may show timestamps and service states

What telemetry does not reliably preserve is a canonicalized, integrity-bound, policy-scoped artifact suitable for independent verification at a later date. When decisions are disputed — in regulatory review, litigation, incident analysis, or institutional audit — partial logs and reconstructed traces are insufficient. Reconstruction from telemetry is inherently interpretive. It is not deterministic proof.

Telemetry Gap
Logs describe activity. They are not integrity-sealed records designed for institutional verification. The distinction matters in post-hoc review.
Disclosure Tension
Institutions require verification without exposure of proprietary weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation. Telemetry-based systems do not resolve this tension.
Reconstruction vs. Proof
Reconstructed traces are inherently interpretive. They are not deterministic. Oversight that relies on reconstruction relies on operator assertions, not verifiable integrity.
Liability Architecture
Absent integrity-bound artifacts, disputes resolve around partial evidence and non-reproducible context. This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.
Institutions require
  • Deterministic structure
  • Integrity sealing
  • Explicit disclosure scope
  • Separation between proprietary internals and oversight verification
[What CEYO is]

A Decoupled Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary — as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway — and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema aligned to review workflows, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals the artifact cryptographically.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

Canonical
Normalized structure. Deterministic byte representation across heterogeneous environments.
Policy-scoped
Content bounded by explicit operator configuration. No implicit expansion beyond declared scope.
Tamper-evident
Cryptographic hash and digital signature enable post-hoc detection of alteration.
Independently verifiable
Verification requires no default exposure of proprietary IP or model internals.
[What CEYO is not]

Records, Not Judgments

  • A model evaluation system
  • A fairness certification system
  • A compliance automation tool
  • A legal adjudication engine
  • A regulatory certification authority

CEYO does not determine whether a decision was lawful, ethical, or fair. CEYO produces integrity-sealed artifacts that institutions may use as part of their oversight processes. Authorized institutions retain responsibility for determinations.

The system is designed around one principle: evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority.

This separation preserves neutrality. It ensures that the evidentiary layer does not become an adjudicator of its own output. It avoids structural conflicts between record generation and policy enforcement.

01 · Policy-Scoped Capture
Define Before You Collect
Operators define what fields are included, excluded, and masked. Capture policy is explicit and versioned. Artifacts reflect declared policy scope; they do not implicitly expand beyond it.
02 · Canonical Structure
Deterministic Normalization
Captured telemetry is normalized into a deterministic JSON artifact schema. Canonicalization is applied in accordance with RFC 8785 (JSON Canonicalization Scheme), producing a stable byte representation independent of serialization order.
03 · Integrity Sealing
Non-Custodial Key Architecture
SHA-256 hashing and ECDSA P-256 digital signature are applied as reference implementations. Private signing keys remain within operator-controlled infrastructure — HSM or KMS environments. CEYO does not require possession, transmission, or storage of private keys.
04 · Constrained Disclosure
Tiered Verification Access
Artifacts may be disclosed under Public / Redacted or Controlled Review tiers. Disclosure tiering allows verification of integrity without default exposure of model weights, proprietary architecture, or trade-secret implementation.
05 · Fail-Open Architecture
Inference Continuity First
Inference continuity remains independent of artifact sealing. If artifact generation fails, inference proceeds. Seal failures are logged and flagged. CEYO is designed not to interrupt operational systems.

Detailed documentation, verification contracts, capture schema specifications, and benchmark methodology are available under controlled disclosure.
