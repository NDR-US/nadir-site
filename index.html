[01] The Accountability Gap

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned — through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation — organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic proof.

Institutions require artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable. Without those properties, oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity.

[02] What CEYO Is

A Decoupled Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO is a decoupled evidentiary layer that attaches beside inference systems and produces policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context.

These artifacts are deterministically structured and cryptographically sealed to support later institutional verification without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

Independent verification
Deterministic procedure executable by authorized third parties.
[03] What CEYO Is Not

Records, Not Judgments

× A model evaluation system
× A fairness certification system
× A compliance automation tool
× A legal adjudication engine
× A regulatory certification authority
CEYO does not determine whether a decision was lawful, ethical, or fair. CEYO produces integrity-sealed artifacts under declared policy scope. Authorized institutions retain responsibility for determinations.

The separation is deliberate. Evidence infrastructure must remain structurally independent from decision authority. Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope — not correctness of output.

CEYO provides the record. Institutions provide the oversight.

01 · Policy-scoped capture

Define before you collect

Operators define included, excluded, and masked fields before artifact generation begins. Policy is versioned and embedded within artifacts, making capture boundaries independently reviewable.

02 · Canonical structure

Deterministic normalization

Captured context is normalized into a deterministic JSON schema. Canonicalization per RFC 8785 produces stable bytes to support reproducible hashing across environments.

03 · Integrity sealing

Hash and signature

After canonicalization, hashing and signatures are applied under a deployment-defined suite. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence; signatures establish origin authenticity.

04 · Non-custodial keys

You control the signing

Signing keys remain inside the operator-controlled environment (HSM, KMS, or TEE). CEYO does not possess or store private signing keys.

05 · Constrained disclosure

Tiered verification access

Artifacts support public/redacted and controlled review tiers. Verification can be performed within declared disclosure boundaries without default exposure of proprietary internals.

06 · Fail-open architecture

Inference continuity first

In fail-open posture, inference continues even if sealing fails. Failures are logged and artifacts are marked as unsealed unless configured otherwise.
