[01] The Accountability Gap

The AccountabilityGap

Modern AI systems generate logs, telemetry, and traces. These signals describe activity. They do not constitute evidence.

Telemetry may show that a model executed It may show input and output It may show timestamps and service states

What telemetry does not reliably preserve is a canonicalized, integrity-bound, policy-scoped artifact suitable for independent verification at a later date. When decisions are disputed — in regulatory review, litigation, incident analysis, or institutional audit — partial logs and reconstructed traces are insufficient. Reconstruction from telemetry is inherently interpretive. It is not deterministic proof. This is the structural deficit.

01 · Telemetry Gap
Activity ≠ Evidence
Logs describe what happened. They are not integrity-sealed records designed for institutional verification.
02 · Disclosure Tension
Verification Without Exposure
Institutions require verification without exposure of proprietary weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation.
03 · Reconstruction vs. Proof
Interpretive ≠ Deterministic
Reconstructed traces are inherently interpretive. Oversight that relies on reconstruction relies on operator assertions, not verifiable integrity.
04 · Liability Architecture
Architectural, Not Operational
Absent integrity-bound artifacts, disputes resolve around partial evidence and non-reproducible context.

[03] What CEYO Is

A DecoupledEvidentiary Substrate

CEYO is a decoupled evidentiary substrate. It attaches at the inference boundary — as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway — and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals cryptographically under a non-custodial key architecture.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

01 · Policy-Scoped Capture
Define Before You Collect
Operators define what fields are included, excluded, and masked before artifact generation begins. Capture policy is explicit and versioned. Artifacts reflect declared policy scope; they do not implicitly expand beyond it. The policy version is recorded within the artifact, making the capture boundary independently reviewable.
02 · Canonical Structure
Deterministic Normalization
Captured telemetry is normalized into a deterministic JSON artifact schema. Canonicalization is applied in accordance with RFC 8785 (JSON Canonicalization Scheme), producing a stable byte representation independent of serialization order. This deterministic structure allows independent recomputation of integrity hashes without interpretive variance.
03 · Integrity Sealing
Hash and Signature
After canonicalization, hashing and digital signatures are applied under a deployment-defined suite. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence; digital signatures establish origin authenticity. The cryptographic suite may evolve as standards evolve. Integrity posture remains constant.
04 · Non-Custodial Keys
You Control the Signing
Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment — HSM, KMS, or TEE. CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys. This non-custodial architecture preserves a clear separation between infrastructure provider and cryptographic authority.
05 · Constrained Disclosure
Tiered Verification Access
Artifacts may be disclosed under Public / Redacted or Controlled Review tiers. Disclosure tiering allows verification of integrity without default exposure of model weights, proprietary architecture, trade-secret implementation, or confidential internals. Verification confirms artifact integrity and policy alignment; it does not expose proprietary content unless authorized.
06 · Fail-Open Architecture
Inference Continuity First
Inference continuity remains independent of artifact sealing. If artifact generation fails, inference proceeds uninterrupted. Failure events are logged and flagged; the artifact is marked as unsealed and the condition becomes reviewable. Fail-open behavior preserves availability unless the operator explicitly configures a stricter posture.
