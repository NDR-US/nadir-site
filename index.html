Evidentiary infrastructure for AI inference systems

NADIR is an attachable integrity and forensic compliance layer that generates policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. Artifacts are sealed with integrity controls to support later verification and structured oversight. NADIR produces verifiable records. It does not adjudicate model correctness.

[STRUCTURAL DEFICIT]

Structural deficits in existing AI logging

Many deployments retain outputs and surface telemetry. They often do not preserve canonical, integrity-bound artifacts suitable for deterministic verification under formal review.

[RECONSTRUCTION]

Post-incident reconstruction

Surface logs describe what occurred. They may not preserve normalized artifacts designed for chain-of-custody handling and later verification.

[CONSTRAINED REVIEW]

Oversight without IP exposure

Institutions require verifiability without default disclosure of model weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation details.

[DISPUTE RISK]

Liability ambiguity

Without integrity-bound artifacts, later disputes rely on partial telemetry and non-reproducible context.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance.
  • Authorized institutions and operators make determinations.
  • Artifacts are policy-scoped, with explicit inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules.
[POSITION]

Record infrastructure, not adjudication

NADIR attaches beside an inference system and produces artifacts structured for independent integrity verification under defined policy.

[CAPTURE]

Normalize defined inference context into a canonical artifact schema aligned to audit workflows.

[INTEGRITY]

Canonicalize deterministically prior to hashing (RFC 8785 for JSON artifacts), then hash and sign for tamper-evidence.

[DISCLOSURE]

Enforce tiered visibility and policy-scoped redaction to support constrained disclosure.

[AUTHORIZED INSTITUTIONS]

Authorized institutions include entities with lawful authority to conduct oversight, compliance review, or regulated operational governance within their jurisdiction.
