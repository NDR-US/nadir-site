NADIR: Deterministic AI Decision-State Recorder

Bridging the accountability gap between observable system inputs and opaque internal inference transformations without exposing proprietary model architecture or weights.

How It Works Document Suite

Reality-bound claim: NADIR is designed to produce deterministic, tamper-evident audit artifacts for review. It does not claim to “explain” model internals or guarantee decision correctness. It claims integrity, reproducibility (under defined conditions), and constrained-disclosure verification.

The problem

Autonomous systems lack structured mechanisms for reconstructing internal decision-state evolution during critical events. This opacity limits post-incident analysis and creates significant liability ambiguity across regulated environments.

Post-incident reconstruction

Review is reduced to surface telemetry rather than decision-state evidence.

Audit & oversight

Independent validation is constrained by trade secret boundaries.

Liability ambiguity

Opaque decisions create uncertainty across regulated and mission-critical deployments.

How it works: three-layer accountability flow

Capture → abstract → sign, producing an audit artifact suitable for verification under constrained disclosure.

Layer 1
Input Baseline Hash
Cryptographic anchoring of structured input state at inference time.
↓
Layer 2
Decision-State Abstraction
Deterministic transformation of internal inference vectors into compliance-safe representations.
Layer 3
NADIR Signature (DWSH)
A tamper-evident cryptographic fingerprint enabling third-party validation without disclosure of proprietary architecture.
↓
Output
Audit / Compliance Artifact
Deterministic integrity record designed for evidentiary preservation and oversight review.
