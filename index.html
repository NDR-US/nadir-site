Evidentiary Infrastructure

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When decisions are reviewed, infrastructure often produces telemetry and logs never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

// BOUNDARY

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput. Accountability is frequently secondary. When a decision is questioned, organizations reconstruct events from partial logs.

Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

• Canonical in structure
• Explicit in capture scope
• Cryptographically sealed
• Independently verifiable

A Decoupled Layer

NADIR operates beside inference engines without modifying model weights. It captures only what policy defines, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals the artifact for tamper-evidence.

Constrained Disclosure

Policy defines inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules. Integrity may be validated without revealing internal mechanics beyond policy scope.
