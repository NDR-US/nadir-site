Evidentiary
Infrastructure
For AI Inference Systems

NADIR is an attachable integrity and forensic compliance layer that generates policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. Artifacts are integrity-sealed to support later verification and structured oversight under constrained disclosure. NADIR produces verifiable records; it does not adjudicate model correctness.

Structural deficits in accountability infrastructure

CARD_A

Telemetry ≠ Evidence

Surface logs describe activity but not deterministic, policy-scoped records suitable for formal scrutiny.

CARD_B

Constrained Disclosure Unresolved

Reviewers need verifiability without seeing proprietary weights, confidential code, or implementation secrets.

CARD_C

Liability Ambiguity

Without sealed artifacts, disputes rely on incomplete telemetry and non-reproducible environment context.

Record infrastructure, not adjudication
