Evidentiary Infrastructure

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, the underlying infrastructure often produces telemetry, logs, and traces that were never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

NADIR is a decoupled evidentiary layer that attaches beside inference systems and produces policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. These artifacts are deterministically structured and cryptographically sealed to support later institutional verification without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

// BOUNDARY

NADIR does not evaluate outputs.
NADIR does not adjudicate compliance.
NADIR produces records.

// DISCLOSURE

Tiered disclosure enables validation without exposure of proprietary internals.

// AVAILABILITY

Fail-open posture preserves inference continuity unless configured otherwise.

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is questioned through regulatory inquiry, audit, incident review, or litigation, organizations reconstruct events from partial logs. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

A Decoupled Verification Layer

NADIR operates beside inference engines without modifying model weights or decision logic. It captures only what policy defines, normalizes that context into canonical structure, applies deterministic canonicalization, and cryptographically seals the artifact.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of output.

NADIR provides the record. Institutions provide oversight.
