Evidentiary infrastructure · Integrity layer · Constrained disclosure

Evidentiary infrastructure for AI inference systems

NADIR is an attachable integrity and forensic compliance layer designed to generate policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. These artifacts are sealed with integrity controls to support later verification and structured oversight.

NADIR does not adjudicate model correctness. It produces verifiable records.

Intended for deployment by regulated operators, institutional oversight bodies, and organizations requiring structured inference accountability. Authorized institutions include entities with lawful authority to conduct oversight, compliance review, or regulated operational governance within their jurisdiction.

Structural deficits in accountability infrastructure

Telemetry isn’t evidence.

Many deployments retain outputs and surface telemetry but do not preserve canonical, integrity-bound artifacts suitable for formal review. This creates a structural gap between deployment and institutional accountability.

Post-incident reconstruction

Surface logs often describe events but do not preserve normalized, integrity-sealed artifacts capable of deterministic verification.

Constrained disclosure

Institutions require verifiability without exposure of proprietary model weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation details.

Liability ambiguity

Absent integrity-bound artifacts, disputes rely on partial telemetry and non-reproducible context.

Weight-neutral verification

NADIR enables verification of provenance and integrity without requiring disclosure of client IP.

What NADIR is

Record infrastructure, not adjudication

NADIR attaches beside an inference system and produces artifacts structured for verification.

Does
  • Normalize defined inference context
  • Canonicalize artifact structure (RFC 8785 for JSON artifacts)
  • Seal artifacts cryptographically (hash + signature)
  • Enable deterministic verification workflows
  • Support controlled disclosure and audit workflows
Does not
  • Adjudicate correctness or “truth”
  • Certify compliance or legality absent written agreement
  • Replace safety engineering or human authorization controls
  • Guarantee inference reproducibility
  • Guarantee semantic equivalence of outputs
Boundary declaration

NADIR does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or policy compliance of AI systems. It generates structured artifacts to support oversight processes conducted by authorized institutions.
