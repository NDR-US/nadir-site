Determinism • Audit integrity • Constrained disclosure

Forensic compliance artifacts for AI inference

NADIR is a deterministic compliance-layer abstraction designed to generate tamper-evident audit artifacts derived from inference-time decision-state representations. It supports oversight, verification, and incident reconstruction workflows without disclosing proprietary model weights, source code, or confidential architecture.

The accountability gap

Many AI systems expose inputs and outputs, but lack deterministic internal state representations that can be preserved and validated as evidentiary-grade artifacts after critical events.

This creates audit friction in regulated deployments and increases ambiguity in post-incident review.

NADIR’s function

NADIR attaches at the inference boundary (gateway, middleware, or service wrapper) to produce a structured artifact: capture → deterministic abstraction → cryptographic signature.

NADIR does not claim decision correctness and does not require disclosure of weights or proprietary implementation.

Core pipeline

Designed for verification under constrained disclosure, not interpretability-by-disclosure.

Layer 1 — Input baseline anchoring

Cryptographic anchoring of structured inference inputs at time t₀.

Layer 2 — Decision-state abstraction

Deterministic transformation of internal vector states into compliance-safe representations.

Layer 3 — NADIR signature (DWSH)

Tamper-evident fingerprint enabling later validation without disclosing proprietary architecture.
