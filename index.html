Reality-bound claim: NADIR is designed to produce deterministic, tamper-evident audit artifacts for review. It does not claim to “explain” model internals or guarantee decision correctness. It claims integrity, reproducibility (under defined conditions), and constrained-disclosure verification.

The problem

Autonomous systems lack structured mechanisms for reconstructing internal decision-state evolution during critical events. This opacity limits post-incident analysis and creates significant liability ambiguity across regulated environments.

Post-incident reconstruction

Review is reduced to surface telemetry rather than decision-state evidence.

Audit & oversight

Independent validation is constrained by trade secret boundaries.

Liability ambiguity

Opaque decisions create uncertainty across regulated and mission-critical deployments.
