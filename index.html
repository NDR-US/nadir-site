01 Structural deficit

The Structural Deficit

Modern AI systems generate logs, telemetry, and traces. These signals describe activity — but they do not constitute evidence.

Telemetry may show that a model executed. It may show input and output. It may show timestamps and service states.

What telemetry cannot provide

A canonicalized, integrity-bound, policy-scoped artifact suitable for independent verification at a later date. Reconstruction is inherently interpretive. Not deterministic proof.

When decisions are disputed — in regulatory review, litigation, incident analysis, or institutional audit — partial logs are insufficient. This is the structural deficit.

Without those properties, accountability relies on trust in system operators rather than verifiable record integrity.

02 What Ceyo is

A Decoupled
Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary — as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway — and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals the result cryptographically.

Canonical
Normalized structure; deterministic byte representation across heterogeneous environments
Policy-scoped
Content bounded by explicit operator configuration; no implicit expansion beyond declared scope
Tamper-evident
Cryptographic hash and digital signature enable post-hoc detection of alteration
Independently verifiable
Verification workflows require no default exposure of proprietary IP or model internals

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

04 Core principle
Evidence infrastructure must remain
separate from decision authority.
CEYO provides the artifact.
Sealed. Canonical. Independently verifiable.
Institutions provide the oversight.
Determinations belong to authorized reviewers.

This separation preserves neutrality. It ensures the evidentiary layer does not become an adjudicator of its own output. It avoids structural conflicts between record generation and policy enforcement.

05 Technical properties

How It Works

01Policy Capture
Operators define included fields, excluded fields, and masking rules. Policy is explicit and versioned. Artifacts reflect declared scope and do not implicitly expand beyond it.
02Canonicalization
JSON artifacts are canonicalized using RFC 8785, producing a stable byte representation independent of serialization order. Required for reproducible hashing across environments.
03Integrity Sealing
SHA-256 hashing and ECDSA P-256 digital signature applied as reference implementations. Private keys remain in operator HSM/KMS — never in CEYO infrastructure. Non-custodial key posture.
04Disclosure Tiers
Public/Redacted tier exposes summary and policy ID only. Controlled Review tier exposes expanded fields to authorized reviewers. Integrity verification requires no exposure of proprietary internals.
05Fail-Open
Inference continuity is independent of artifact sealing. If generation fails, inference proceeds. Seal failures are logged and flagged. CEYO does not interrupt operational systems by default.
