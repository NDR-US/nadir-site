[01] The Accountability Gap

Telemetry Describes Activity.
It Does Not Preserve Proof.

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, the underlying infrastructure often produces telemetry, logs, and traces that were never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry may show that a model executed It may show input and output It may show timestamps and service states

When a decision is questioned—through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation—organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

01 · Canonicality
Activity ≠ Evidence
Logs describe events. They are not integrity-sealed records designed for later independent verification.
02 · Capture Scope
Explicit Boundary
Institutions require clarity about what was captured, excluded, and masked—by policy defined before generation.
03 · Integrity
Reconstruction vs Proof
Reconstructed traces are interpretive. Deterministic artifacts enable reproducible integrity validation.
04 · Independence
Operator Assertions
Without independently verifiable artifacts, oversight depends on operator claims rather than verifiable integrity.
[02] A Decoupled Verification Layer

Records,
Not Judgments

NADIR introduces an evidentiary substrate that operates beside inference engines as a sidecar, gateway, or wrapper. It captures only what policy defines, normalizes captured context into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals artifacts cryptographically for tamper-evidence.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of output.

01 · Canonical
Deterministic Structure
Normalized schema and stable byte representation independent of serialization order.
02 · Policy-Scoped
Explicit Capture Boundary
Inclusion, exclusion, masking defined before generation. Policy version embedded in artifacts.
03 · Sealed
Tamper-Evident Integrity
Canonicalization + hash + signature establish alteration detection and provenance claims.
04 · Verifiable
No IP Exposure Required
Independent validation using public keys—without default disclosure of proprietary internals.
[03] Boundaries

Infrastructure,
Not Adjudication

× A model evaluation system
× A compliance automation tool
× A fairness certification system
× A legal adjudication engine
× A regulatory authority

NADIR does not determine whether a decision was lawful, ethical, or fair. NADIR produces integrity-sealed artifacts that institutions may use within oversight processes. Authorized institutions retain responsibility for all determinations.

Evidence infrastructure must remain structurally independent from decision authority. This separation preserves neutrality, prevents conflicts of interest, and ensures the evidentiary layer does not become an adjudicator of its own output.

NADIR provides the record. Institutions provide the oversight.
