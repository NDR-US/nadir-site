[01] The Accountability Gap

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned — through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation — organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions require more than reconstructed context. They require artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable. Without those properties, oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity.

01 · Policy-scoped capture

Define before you collect

Artifact generation begins with explicit policy definition. Policy defines which fields are included, excluded, and masked, and records policy identifier and version inside the artifact.

02 · Canonical structure

Deterministic normalization

Captured context is normalized into a canonical artifact schema. JSON canonicalization under RFC 8785 produces stable bytes for reproducible hashing and verification.

03 · Cryptographic sealing

Hash and signature

After canonicalization, artifacts are hashed and digitally signed under a deployment-defined suite. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence. Signatures establish origin authenticity.

04 · Non-custodial key architecture

Operator-controlled custody

Signing keys are generated and retained within the operator-controlled environment (HSM, KMS, or TEE). CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.

05 · Constrained disclosure

Tiered access by design

Artifacts support tiered disclosure models. Integrity may be verified without exposing proprietary internals beyond declared policy scope. Boundaries are defined pre-generation.

06 · Integrity–availability balance

Fail-open posture

CEYO does not introduce a default dependency that halts inference. In fail-open configuration, inference continues if sealing fails; failures are logged and artifacts are marked accordingly.

Boundary Declaration  ·  V2026.02.19

CEYO produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance outcomes.

CEYO does not certify regulatory compliance.

CEYO does not guarantee legal admissibility.

CEYO does not guarantee inference reproducibility.

Authorized institutions and operators retain responsibility for determinations.

Cryptographic suite references are illustrative; deployments select suites appropriate to context.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope — not semantic correctness of output.
