Evidentiary Infrastructure

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, underlying infrastructure often produces logs that were never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput and latency. Accountability is secondary.

When a decision is questioned, organizations reconstruct events from partial logs. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

Decoupled Verification

NADIR captures only what policy defines, normalizes that context, and cryptographically seals the artifact.

NADIR provides the record.
Institutions provide oversight.
