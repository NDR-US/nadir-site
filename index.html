NADIR: evidentiary infrastructure for AI inference

NADIR is an attachable forensic compliance layer designed to generate policy-scoped, canonical inference artifacts and seal them with cryptographic integrity controls—so organizations can support later verification and structured oversight without disclosing proprietary model weights, source code, or confidential architecture.

The accountability gap

Many AI deployments retain outputs and surface telemetry, but lack a canonical, integrity-sealed record designed for formal review workflows. When an event is disputed later, the absence of verifiable artifacts creates uncertainty across incident reconstruction, oversight, and liability.

Reconstruction limits

Logs may describe what happened, but not preserve a record designed for verification under defined evidentiary boundaries.

Constrained disclosure

Institutions need reviewability without forcing exposure of trade secrets or confidential implementation details.

Dispute posture

Without integrity controls and canonicalization, records are easier to contest and harder to validate.

How NADIR works (high level)

NADIR follows a strict mechanics chain designed for audit workflows.

1) Capture (policy-scoped)

Attach at the inference boundary and capture only what policy defines: included, excluded, and masked fields.

2) Canonicalize (schema)

Normalize captured context into a canonical artifact format to reduce ambiguity and support repeatable verification.

3) Seal (integrity)

Hash and sign the canonical artifact to support authenticity validation and tamper detection.

4) Verify (workflow)

Independent reviewers recompute and validate integrity fields under defined conditions—without requiring IP disclosure.

Boundary Declaration
  • NADIR is not a regulator, certification authority, or compliance determination engine.
  • NADIR does not guarantee correctness, safety, fairness, legality, or policy compliance of AI decisions.
  • NADIR generates integrity-sealed artifacts to support verification and oversight workflows conducted by authorized reviewers.
  • NADIR does not require disclosure of proprietary weights, source code, or confidential architecture.
