[01] The Structural Deficit

The Structural
Deficit

Modern AI systems generate logs, telemetry, and traces. These signals describe activity. They do not constitute evidence.

Telemetry may show that a model executed
It may show input and output
It may show timestamps and service states

What telemetry does not reliably preserve is a canonicalized, integrity-bound, policy-scoped artifact suitable for independent verification at a later date. When decisions are disputed — in regulatory review, litigation, incident analysis, or institutional audit — partial logs and reconstructed traces are insufficient. Reconstruction from telemetry is inherently interpretive. It is not deterministic proof. This is the structural deficit.

01 · Telemetry Gap
Activity ≠ Evidence
Logs describe what happened. They are not integrity-sealed records designed for institutional verification. That distinction matters in post-hoc review, litigation, and audit contexts.
02 · Disclosure Tension
Verification Without Exposure
Institutions require verification without exposure of proprietary weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation. Telemetry-based systems do not resolve this structural tension.
03 · Reconstruction vs. Proof
Interpretive ≠ Deterministic
Reconstructed traces are inherently interpretive. They are not deterministic. Oversight that relies on reconstruction relies on operator assertions, not verifiable record integrity.
04 · Liability Architecture
Architectural, Not Operational
Absent integrity-bound artifacts, disputes resolve around partial evidence and non-reproducible context. This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight correctable by better logging.
Without these properties, accountability relies on operator trust — not verifiable record integrity

[02] What CEYO Is

A Decoupled
Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO is a decoupled evidentiary substrate. It attaches at the inference boundary — as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway — and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization per RFC 8785, and seals cryptographically under a non-custodial key architecture.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

01 · Canonical
Deterministic Structure
Normalized schema. Stable byte representation independent of serialization order or deployment environment.
02 · Policy-Scoped
Explicit Capture Boundary
Content defined by operator before capture. No implicit expansion. Policy version recorded in artifact.
03 · Tamper-Evident
Cryptographic Sealing
Hash and digital signature establish that an artifact has not been altered since the sealing event.
04 · Independently Verifiable
No IP Exposure Required
Verification workflows require only public keys. Proprietary internals remain undisclosed by default.
[03] What CEYO Is Not

Records,
Not Judgments

CEYO does not determine whether a decision was lawful, ethical, or fair. CEYO produces integrity-sealed artifacts that institutions may use as part of their oversight processes. Authorized institutions retain responsibility for all determinations.

01 · Policy-Scoped Capture
Define Before You Collect
Operators define what fields are included, excluded, and masked before artifact generation begins. Capture policy is explicit and versioned; artifacts reflect declared policy scope without implicit expansion.
02 · Canonical Structure
Deterministic Normalization
Captured telemetry is normalized into a deterministic JSON artifact schema. Canonicalization per RFC 8785 produces a stable byte representation independent of serialization order and environment.
03 · Integrity Sealing
Hash and Signature
After canonicalization, cryptographic hashing and digital signatures are applied under a deployment-defined suite. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence; signatures establish authenticity.
04 · Non-Custodial Keys
You Control the Signing
Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment — HSM, KMS, or TEE. CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.
05 · Constrained Disclosure
Tiered Verification Access
Artifacts may be disclosed under public/redacted or controlled-review tiers. Verification confirms integrity under declared policy scope without default exposure of proprietary internals.
06 · Fail-Open Architecture
Inference Continuity First
Inference continuity remains independent of sealing. If artifact generation fails, inference proceeds uninterrupted; failure events are logged and the condition becomes reviewable.
