[01] The Accountability Gap

The Accountability
Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned — through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation — organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions require more than reconstructed context. They require artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable. Without those properties, oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity.

This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

[02] What CEYO Is

A Decoupled
Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary — as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway — and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization (RFC 8785), and seals cryptographically under a non-custodial key architecture.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

[03] Technical Properties

Capture. Structure.
Seal. Verify.

Capture (policy-scoped): Operators define included, excluded, and masked fields before generation. Scope is explicit and versioned within the artifact.

Structure (canonical schema): Captured context is normalized into a deterministic artifact schema designed for review — not free-form logs.

Canonicalization (RFC 8785): Logically equivalent artifacts produce identical byte representations across environments — required for reproducible hashing.

Sealing (hash + signature): Hashing establishes tamper-evidence; signatures establish origin authenticity. Cryptographic suites remain deployment-defined.

Keys (non-custodial): Private signing keys remain under operator control (HSM/KMS/TEE). CEYO does not possess or store private keys.

Disclosure (tiered): Policy defines public/redacted vs controlled-review tiers before generation. Integrity can be validated without forced IP exposure.

Availability (fail-open by default): Inference continuity remains independent. Seal failures are logged, artifacts marked unsealed, and conditions become reviewable.

Verification (independent): Canonicalize → recompute hash → validate signature → check policy alignment. Output states: Confirmed / Failed / Policy Mismatch.
