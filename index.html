Evidentiary infrastructure · Integrity layer · Constrained disclosure

Evidentiary infrastructure for AI inference systems

NADIR is an attachable integrity and forensic compliance layer designed to generate policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. Artifacts are sealed with integrity controls to support later verification and structured oversight.

NADIR does not adjudicate model correctness. It produces verifiable records. Intended for deployment by regulated operators, institutional oversight bodies, and organizations requiring structured inference accountability.

Authorized institutions include entities with lawful authority to conduct oversight, compliance review, or regulated operational governance within their jurisdiction.

Structural deficits in accountability infrastructure

Deployment without institutional accountability

AI inference systems are optimized for performance and scale. Many deployments retain outputs and surface telemetry but do not preserve canonical, integrity-bound artifacts suitable for formal review. This creates a structural gap between deployment and institutional accountability.

Post-incident reconstruction

Surface logs often describe events but do not preserve normalized, integrity-sealed artifacts capable of deterministic verification.

Constrained disclosure

Institutions require verifiability without exposure of proprietary model weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation details.

Liability ambiguity

Absent integrity-bound artifacts, disputes rely on partial telemetry and non-reproducible context.

Weight-neutral verification

NADIR is designed to preserve client trade-secret boundaries while enabling artifact verification under defined policy.

Boundary declaration
  • • NADIR does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or policy compliance of AI systems.
  • • It generates structured artifacts to support oversight processes conducted by authorized institutions.
