[01] The Accountability Gap

The Accountability
Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned—through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation—organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions require more than reconstructed context. They require artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable.

Without those properties, oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity. This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

[02] What CEYO Is

A Decoupled
Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary—as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway—and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization (RFC 8785), and seals cryptographically under a non-custodial key architecture.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

[04] How It Works

Technical
Properties

01 · Capture 02 · Structure 03 · Sealing 04 · Keys 05 · Disclosure 06 · Availability
01 · Policy-Scoped Capture
Define Before You Collect
Operators define what fields are included, excluded, and masked before artifact generation begins. Capture policy is explicit and versioned. Artifacts reflect declared policy scope; they do not implicitly expand beyond it.
02 · Canonical Structure
Deterministic Normalization
Captured telemetry is normalized into a deterministic JSON artifact schema. Canonicalization is applied in accordance with RFC 8785, producing a stable byte representation independent of serialization order.
03 · Integrity Sealing
Hash and Signature
After canonicalization, hashing and digital signature are applied as reference implementations. Suite selection is configurable. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence; signatures establish origin authenticity.
04 · Non-Custodial Keys
You Control the Signing
Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment—HSM, KMS, or TEE. CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.
05 · Constrained Disclosure
Tiered Verification Access
Artifacts may be disclosed under Public / Redacted or Controlled Review tiers. Integrity may be validated without default exposure of weights, proprietary architecture, or trade-secret implementation.
06 · Fail-Open Architecture
Inference Continuity First
Inference continuity remains independent of artifact sealing. If artifact generation fails, inference proceeds uninterrupted. Failure events are logged and reviewable unless the operator configures a stricter posture.
Controlled Review

Detailed documentation, verification contracts, capture schema specifications, and benchmark methodology are available under controlled disclosure. Access is granted following review request evaluation.
