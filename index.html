Executive Summary

CEYO is a decoupled evidentiary layer that attaches beside inference systems and produces policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. These artifacts are deterministically structured and cryptographically sealed to support later institutional verification without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned—through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation—organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions require more than reconstructed context. They require artifacts that are:

  • Canonical in structure
  • Explicit in capture scope
  • Cryptographically sealed
  • Independently verifiable

Without those properties, oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity. This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

Constrained Disclosure
Validation without exposure
Oversight frequently requires validation without exposure of proprietary architecture, confidential model weights, or trade-secret implementation. Artifacts support tiered disclosure. Policy defines inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules before generation.
Availability by Design
Fail-open posture
Artifact sealing failures may be logged and flagged without interrupting inference unless explicitly configured otherwise. Availability remains an institutional decision.
Institutional Position
Infrastructure, not outcomes
CEYO does not certify regulatory compliance. It does not guarantee admissibility. It does not determine fairness or legality. It produces records. Institutions determine their meaning.

DECOUPLED VERIFICATION ARCHITECTURE

Verification Substrate
At the Inference Boundary

CEYO is designed as a verification substrate that operates beside AI inference systems without modifying model weights or core decision logic.

Its purpose is narrow and explicit: to generate deterministic, integrity-sealed artifacts representing defined inference-time context under policy scope.

It does not evaluate model outputs. It does not intervene in inference decisions. It does not alter model behavior.

This separation preserves architectural neutrality while enabling institutional review.

Attachment at the Inference Boundary

CEYO integrates at the inference boundary through a sidecar, gateway, or API wrapper configuration.

Attachment at the boundary allows CEYO to observe defined inference events, capture policy-scoped context, normalize event structure, produce deterministic artifacts, and seal artifacts without interfering with decision flow.

By remaining external to model internals, CEYO reduces operational friction and preserves system integrity.

Policy-Scoped Capture
Define before you collect
Artifact generation begins with explicit policy definition. Only fields defined within policy scope are captured. Capture scope is versioned and recorded within the artifact itself.
Canonical Structure
Deterministic records
Captured context is normalized into a canonical artifact schema designed for deterministic integrity verification. Canonical artifacts are structured records intended for review—not free-form logs.
RFC 8785
Deterministic canonicalization
Before hashing, JSON artifacts are canonicalized using RFC 8785 (JSON Canonicalization Scheme), ensuring logically equivalent structures produce identical byte representations across environments.
Cryptographic Sealing
Hash + signature
After canonicalization, artifacts are sealed using hashing and digital signatures under a deployment-defined suite. Reference: SHA-256 and ECDSA (P-256). Suite selection remains configurable.
Non-Custodial Keys
Authority stays institutional
Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment (HSM, KMS, TEE). CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.
Independent Verification
Deterministic workflow
Verification includes RFC 8785 canonicalization, hash recomputation, signature validation, and policy identifier/version alignment checks. Outcomes: Integrity Confirmed, Integrity Failed, Policy Scope Mismatch.

OPERATIONAL INTEGRATION

Attach. Configure. Seal.
Preserve Autonomy.

CEYO integrates without requiring modification of model weights or inference logic. It operates as an attachable integrity layer at the inference boundary, enabling deterministic artifact generation while preserving system performance and autonomy.

Deployment Model
  • Sidecar process alongside inference service
  • Gateway layer intercepting inference requests
  • API wrapper around inference calls

All deployment modes preserve the same principle: the inference engine remains unchanged. The integrity layer operates externally.

Artifact Lifecycle
  1. Policy-scoped fields are captured
  2. Context is normalized into canonical schema
  3. JSON canonicalization is applied
  4. Artifact is hashed
  5. Artifact is digitally signed
  6. Artifact is stored under retention posture
Retention & Chain-of-Custody Operator governed
Retention duration is not prescribed. Operators define retention policies consistent with privacy law, regulatory requirements, and governance frameworks. CEYO supports retrieval logging, re-verification, disclosure tiers, and review-ready formatting. Institutional handling remains operator responsibility.
Performance Considerations
Minimal incremental overhead
Overhead depends on capture scope size, crypto suite selection, hardware environment, and storage architecture. Sealing operates independently of inference execution. In fail-open configuration, inference continuity is preserved even if sealing encounters failure.
Boundaries of Responsibility
Infrastructure only
CEYO does not replace safety engineering frameworks, human authorization controls, inference correctness validation, or regulatory sufficiency determinations. Deployment responsibility remains with the implementing organization. Oversight determinations remain institutional.

ROADMAP

Determinism First.
Expansion Later.

CEYO is being developed deliberately. Integrity infrastructure must be deterministic before it can be expanded.

The roadmap prioritizes canonical artifact stability and verification correctness before feature expansion. Claims remain proportional to implemented functionality at each phase.

Phase 1 Canonical schema + prototype sealing
Focus: policy-scoped capture definition, canonical schema stabilization, RFC 8785 canonicalization, cryptographic sealing, non-custodial key integration, fail-open availability validation. Objective is determinism, not breadth.
Phase 2 Independent verification harness
Focus: standalone verification CLI, public-key validation workflow, policy alignment checks, automated integrity status reporting, cross-environment verification consistency testing. Verification must be executable independently.
Phase 3 Chain-of-custody workflow support
Focus: artifact retrieval logging posture, structured retention metadata, review-ready verification summaries, incident-scoped artifact bundling, disclosure tier enforcement validation. CEYO supports workflows; institutions define procedures.
Phase 4 Operational hardening
Potential: performance optimization, deployment automation tooling, broader crypto suite support, extended regulatory crosswalk mappings, enhanced audit logging instrumentation—only after determinism remains stable across prior phases.
What the Roadmap Does Not Include
Fairness evaluation engines, model correctness validation, automated regulatory certification, real-time intervention systems, behavioral analytics modules. CEYO remains intentionally narrow in scope.
No delivery dates are publicly committed. Each phase is validated before expansion.

OVERSIGHT PORTAL

Verification is Mechanical.
Oversight is Institutional.

CEYO artifacts are designed to support structured oversight workflows. This page describes how regulated operators, authorized institutions, and independent auditors interact with artifacts generated under defined policy scope.

CEYO does not conduct oversight. It produces records that may be reviewed. Oversight authority remains institutional.

Regulated Operator
Deploys + defines policy
Configures capture scope, manages policy IDs/versions, maintains retention posture, manages signing key custody, and responds to authorized review requests. CEYO does not override operator authority.
Authorized Institution
Verifies + determines meaning
Performs RFC 8785 canonicalization, hash recomputation, signature validation, and policy alignment verification. Determines evidentiary sufficiency, regulatory implications, compliance conclusions, and admissibility posture. CEYO does not.
Independent Forensic Auditor
Validates integrity
Where authorized, auditors retrieve incident-scoped artifacts, execute independent verification, document chain-of-custody handling, and produce institutional reports. Auditors validate integrity and provenance; they do not re-evaluate inference correctness via CEYO.
Verification Workflow
  1. Retrieve artifact under authorized access
  2. Canonicalize using RFC 8785 (JSON Canonicalization Scheme)
  3. Recompute cryptographic hash
  4. Validate digital signature against operator public key
  5. Confirm Policy ID and version alignment
Integrity Confirmed
Hash + signature validate
Integrity Failed
Mismatch or validation failure
Policy Scope Mismatch
Unexpected policy ID/version

GOVERNANCE POSTURE

Boundaries First.
Determinism Always.

CEYO is designed to support accountable review while minimizing unnecessary collection and preserving institutional boundaries.

Governance here refers to structural design principles embedded in the integrity layer itself — not external regulatory authority.

CEYO produces records. Institutions make determinations.

1. Minimization by Design
Policy-defined capture
Capture scope is explicitly defined by policy prior to generation. Only fields declared within policy are recorded. Policy IDs and version numbers are embedded within artifacts to make boundaries reviewable. Minimization is structural, not aspirational.
2. Controlled Disclosure
Verification without forced exposure
Artifacts enable integrity verification without disclosing model weights, training datasets, or source code. Disclosure tiers are defined before generation. Redaction is policy-defined, not improvised post hoc.
3. Deterministic Auditability
Reproducible verification
Artifacts are canonicalized and sealed deterministically, enabling reproducible validation across heterogeneous environments. Verification is mechanical. Interpretation remains institutional.
4. Non-Adjudication
Infrastructure only
CEYO does not determine correctness, compliance, lawfulness, or fairness. It provides integrity-sealed records. Oversight conclusions remain with authorized institutions.
Alignment Posture
Governance design is informed by contemporary integrity-oriented principles consistent with structured record-keeping, version control discipline, minimization, purpose limitation, and separation of verification from adjudication. References to external frameworks are descriptive only.
CEYO does not claim endorsement, certification, or formal alignment by any regulatory body or standards institution.

TRUST INFRASTRUCTURE

Trust is Mechanics.
Not Statements.

Trust in evidentiary systems is established through verifiable mechanics. CEYO’s trust posture is anchored in determinism, non-custodial cryptographic architecture, and explicit boundaries.

Trust here refers to integrity properties — not outcome validation.

Integrity Architecture
Deterministic sealing pipeline
Controls include RFC 8785 canonicalization, cryptographic hashing, digital signatures under deployment-defined suite, non-custodial key custody, policy ID/version embedding, and explicit disclosure tier designation. Verification is reproducible and independent of proprietary internals.
Non-Custodial Keys
Authority remains institutional
Signing keys remain under operator control. CEYO does not generate private keys by default, retain private keys, transmit private signing material, or assume cryptographic authority. Liability boundaries remain clear.
Supply Chain Transparency
SBOM posture
CEYO maintains SBOM for reference builds. Supported formats include SPDX and CycloneDX. SBOM sharing occurs under controlled review and does not disclose proprietary architecture beyond agreed scope.
Vulnerability Disclosure
Responsible reporting channel
Maintains a structured VDP to support responsible reporting. Good-faith research within defined boundaries is triaged. Unauthorized access is not permitted. No bounty commitment is made.
Trust Boundaries
CEYO does not guarantee immunity from attack, regulatory certification, legal sufficiency, or operational correctness. Trust posture is defined by deterministic architecture and explicit limitations.
