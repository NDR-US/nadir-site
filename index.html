Problem

Autonomous and AI-enabled systems often expose inputs and outputs but not the internal inference-state transformations that led to decisions. This limits auditability, incident reconstruction, and oversight in regulated environments.

Accountability gap

Internal logic paths are typically opaque during review, creating audit and liability ambiguity.

Forensic constraints

Post-event reconstruction is reduced to surface telemetry rather than decision-state evidence.

Oversight friction

Trade secret boundaries complicate third-party validation and compliance verification.

Architecture

The system is positioned as a deterministic overlay: capture → transform → sign, producing a compliance-safe fingerprint suitable for audit and evidentiary preservation.

Layer 1
Input Baseline Hash
Deterministic cryptographic anchoring of structured inference inputs at time t₀.
↓
Layer 2
Decision-State Abstraction
Deterministic transformation of inference vectors into compliance-safe representations.
Layer 3
NADIR Signature (DWSH)
Tamper-evident, collision-resistant state fingerprint for third-party verification without IP exposure.
↓
Output
Independent Audit / Oversight
Verification of decision-state integrity and traceability with preserved proprietary boundaries.
