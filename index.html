Evidentiary
Infrastructure
for Autonomous Systems

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, the underlying infrastructure often produces telemetry, logs, and traces that were never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity. It does not preserve proof.

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned—through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation—organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions require more than reconstructed context. They require artifacts that are:

  • Canonical in structure
  • Explicit in capture scope
  • Cryptographically sealed
  • Independently verifiable

Without those properties, oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity. This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

A Decoupled Verification Layer

CEYO introduces an evidentiary substrate that operates beside inference engines. It does not modify model weights. It does not intervene in decision logic. It captures only what policy defines, normalizes that context into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals the artifact for tamper-evidence.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of output.

CEYO does not

evaluate outputs, adjudicate compliance, certify legality, or determine fairness.

CEYO does

produce deterministic, integrity-sealed records under explicit policy scope.

Constrained Disclosure

Oversight frequently requires validation without exposure of proprietary architecture, confidential model weights, or trade-secret implementation.

Artifacts support tiered disclosure. Policy defines inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules before artifact generation. Disclosure boundaries are explicit rather than implied.

Availability by Design

CEYO does not introduce a default operational dependency that halts inference. Artifact sealing failures may be logged and flagged without interrupting inference unless explicitly configured otherwise. Availability remains an institutional decision.

Institutional Position

CEYO is infrastructure. It is not adjudication.

  • It does not certify regulatory compliance.
  • It does not guarantee admissibility.
  • It does not determine fairness or legality.

CEYO provides the record. Institutions provide the oversight.
