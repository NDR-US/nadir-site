Record · Seal · Verify

Record

Capture is policy-scoped. Only declared fields are collected. Out-of-scope data is excluded by design.

Seal

Canonicalize deterministically and seal cryptographically so post-hoc alteration is detectable.

Verify

Independent parties recompute canonicalization and validate signatures to confirm integrity and provenance.

Purpose

Designed for fast executive clarity while remaining credible for technical and security reviewers.

Five steps

The mechanics chain is fixed. Each step produces a verifiable boundary.

01 · Attach
Deploy as a layer

Sidecar, gateway, or wrapper at the inference boundary.

02 · Capture
Define scope first

Policy-scoped fields only. Masking applied before sealing.

03 · Canonicalize
Normalize deterministically

RFC 8785 yields reproducible bytes across environments.

04 · Seal
Tamper-evident integrity

Hash + signature under operator key custody (non-custodial).

05 · Verify
Independent validation

Recompute canonicalization + validate signature + policy alignment.

Example
policy_id: "POL-001" policy_version: "V1" canonicalization: "RFC8785" hash_algorithm: "SHA-256" signature_algorithm: "ECDSA-P256"

Paste a sample artifact to see the verification steps and basic format checks. Cryptographic verification is performed in the verifier toolkit.

Verification walkthrough
Status · waiting
1) Canonicalize under the recorded scheme RFC 8785 JSON canonicalization
2) Recompute hash over canonical bytes SHA-256 digest recomputation
3) Validate signature against public key reference Origin authenticity + tamper evidence
4) Confirm policy alignment (ID/version/hash) Scope boundary confirmation

Successful verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope. It does not confirm output correctness, legality, or compliance conclusions.

Evidence flow

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope — not correctness of model output.
