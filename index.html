Independent evidentiary infrastructure

Evidentiary Infrastructure for AI Systems

CEYO is an independent evidentiary layer that generates policy-scoped, cryptographically sealed records from AI-supported operations, enabling later institutional verification without modifying the originating system.

Boundary

CEYO operates outside the model. It does not modify, instrument, or interfere with AI inference. It records only what a governing capture policy explicitly permits.

Objective

Why this layer exists.

AI systems increasingly influence consequential outcomes: credit, employment, healthcare, benefits, autonomous control. In regulated and high-stakes contexts, there is a distinct requirement that cannot be met by internal logging alone: the ability to demonstrate that a system behaved as declared, to an external party, without disclosing what it decided.

  • CEYO addresses this gap.
  • A sealed artifact proves that a record was produced at a specific time, under a specific policy, and has not been altered since — without revealing what the underlying system processed or concluded.
  • The focus is evidentiary integrity, not narrative assertion.
Overview structure

Record · Seal · Verify

CEYO operates as a neutral evidentiary layer attached to an AI inference boundary. It captures policy-scoped decision records and produces deterministic, cryptographically sealed artifacts that can be independently verified.

Record

Policy-scoped capture

Recording only fields permitted by an explicit, declared capture policy.

Seal

Deterministic canonicalization and cryptographic integrity

Serialization that produces identical bytes for identical inputs, followed by hashing and digital signatures that make post-hoc alteration detectable.

Verify

Independent verification

Recomputing the hash and validating the signature using only the artifact and a public key.

Disclosure

Constrained disclosure

Sharing verifiable evidence of behavior without exposing proprietary model internals.

Mechanics

Five operational stages.

The mechanism is intentionally narrow: capture what has been declared, canonicalize it deterministically, seal it cryptographically, and make it independently verifiable without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

01 · Attach
Deploy at the inference boundary
CEYO is positioned as a sidecar, gateway, or wrapper so the capture boundary is explicit and auditable.
02 · Capture
Apply policy-defined capture rules
Only policy-scoped fields are included. Exclusions and masking occur before sealing.
03 · Canonicalize
Serialize deterministically
The captured body is serialized using RFC 8785, producing identical bytes for identical inputs regardless of key ordering or whitespace.
04 · Seal
Hash and sign the artifact
A SHA-256 hash and an ECDSA P-256 signature are computed over the canonical bytes.
05 · Verify
Recompute and confirm
Any party with the sealed artifact and the corresponding public key can recompute the hash and validate the signature, with no access to the originating system.
Canonicalization
RFC 8785

Hashing

Integrity comparison against the recorded artifact body.

Cryptographic provenance without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

Independent

Mechanical recomputation, not narrative trust in the originating system.

Boundary statement

What CEYO does not claim.

CEYO is an evidentiary infrastructure. It is not a truth engine, a fairness engine, a compliance certification, or a model auditor.

Category Boundary
Truth engine CEYO does not verify that an AI decision was correct.
Fairness engine CEYO does not assess whether a decision was equitable or unbiased.
Compliance certification CEYO does not certify conformance with any regulatory standard.
Model auditor CEYO does not access, analyze, or evaluate model internals.
  • CEYO proves that a sealed record is unaltered and was produced under a declared policy.
  • It does not prove anything about the quality, legality, or appropriateness of the underlying AI output.
