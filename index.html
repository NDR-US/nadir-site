Evidentiary Infrastructure · For Autonomous Systems · V2026.02.19

Go Beyond Telemetry

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, the underlying infrastructure often produces telemetry, logs, and traces that were never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity. It does not preserve proof.
CEYO exists to address that structural gap.

Position

A Decoupled Evidentiary Layer

CEYO attaches beside inference systems and produces policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. These artifacts are deterministically structured and cryptographically sealed to support later institutional verification without default disclosure of proprietary internals.

CEYO
does not evaluate outputs or intervene in decision logic.
CEYO
does not adjudicate compliance, legality, or correctness.
CEYO
produces records. Institutions provide oversight.
The Gap

The Accountability Deficit

Modern deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary. When a decision is later questioned—through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation—organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Required
Canonical structure · Explicit capture scope · Cryptographic sealing · Independent verification
Without
Oversight depends on operator assertions rather than verifiable integrity.
Disclosure

Constrained Verification

Oversight frequently requires validation without exposure of proprietary architecture, confidential weights, or trade-secret implementation. CEYO artifacts support tiered disclosure. Policy defines inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules before generation. Disclosure boundaries are explicit rather than implied.

Continuity

Availability by Design

CEYO does not introduce a default operational dependency that halts inference. Sealing failures may be logged and flagged without interrupting inference unless explicitly configured otherwise. Availability remains an institutional decision.

Position

Institutional Boundaries

CEYO is infrastructure. It is not adjudication. It does not certify regulatory compliance, guarantee admissibility, or determine fairness or legality. Authorized institutions and regulated operators retain responsibility for determinations.
