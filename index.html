Go Beyond Telemetry

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, the underlying infrastructure often produces telemetry, logs, and traces that were never designed to function as durable evidence.

Structural gap

Telemetry describes activity. It does not preserve proof.

When a decision is later questioned—through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation— organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Narrow purpose

CEYO produces records. Institutions provide oversight.

CEYO attaches beside inference systems and produces policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. These artifacts are deterministically structured and cryptographically sealed for later institutional verification under constrained disclosure.

A Decoupled Verification Layer

Architectural neutrality by design.

CEYO operates beside inference engines. It does not modify model weights. It does not intervene in decision logic. It captures only what policy defines, normalizes that context into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals the artifact for tamper-evidence.

Boundary

What CEYO does not do

  • Does not evaluate outputs
  • Does not adjudicate compliance
  • Does not guarantee admissibility
  • Does not certify legality or correctness
Constrained disclosure

Verification without default exposure.

Oversight frequently requires validation without exposure of proprietary architecture, confidential model weights, or trade-secret implementation. Artifacts support tiered disclosure. Integrity may be validated within declared scope.

Availability discipline

Fail-open by default.

Artifact sealing failures may be logged and flagged without interrupting inference unless explicitly configured otherwise. Availability remains an institutional decision.

Institutional Position

Infrastructure, not adjudication.

CEYO provides deterministic artifacts representing defined inference-time context under policy scope. Institutions determine their meaning. Oversight authority remains institutional.
