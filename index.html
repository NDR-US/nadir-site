Evidentiary Substrate for Autonomous Systems

NADIR attaches beside inference systems to produce canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context. Artifacts are sealed for tamper-evidence and structured for constrained, institutional verification without default IP exposure.

[STRUCTURAL DEFICIT]

Structural deficits in existing AI logging

Telemetry can describe activity, but it often fails to preserve reviewable evidence: canonicalized records with explicit scope, custody-ready integrity metadata, and deterministic structures suitable for later verification.

[RECONSTRUCTION]

Post-incident reconstruction

Surface logs do not reliably preserve normalized, integrity-bound artifacts designed for custody handling and later verification.

[CONSTRAINED REVIEW]

Oversight without IP exposure

Institutions require verifiability without default disclosure of model weights, confidential architecture, or trade-secret implementation.

[DISPUTE RISK]

Liability ambiguity

Without integrity-bound artifacts, later disputes rely on partial telemetry and non-reproducible context.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or compliance outcomes.
  • Authorized institutions and operators make determinations and retain responsibility for decisions.
  • Artifacts are policy-scoped with explicit inclusion, exclusion, and masking rules.
[DECOUPLED VERIFICATION]

The NADIR sidecar model

NADIR attaches beside an inference system and produces artifacts structured for independent integrity verification under defined policy scope. The design intent is weight-neutral operation and constrained disclosure for institutional workflows.

[CAPTURE]

Policy-scoped capture

Operators define the canonical scope: what is included, what is excluded, and what is masked.

[NORMALIZE]

Canonical structure

Telemetry is normalized into a stable artifact schema aligned to review and reconstruction workflows.

[SEAL]

Deterministic integrity

JSON artifacts are canonicalized (RFC 8785) prior to hashing, then hashed and signed to support tamper-evidence.

[KEY CUSTODY]

NADIR is designed for non-custodial key posture: signing keys are generated and retained within the operator’s controlled environment (e.g., HSM/KMS). NADIR does not require possession of private keys for artifact verification workflows.
