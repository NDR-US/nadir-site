The Accountability Gap

Modern deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Oversight infrastructure is often secondary. When an outcome is later disputed — internal audit, incident reconstruction, litigation, or institutional review — organizations reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces.

Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic proof. Institutions require artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable under constrained disclosure.

Core principle

Evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority. CEYO provides the artifact; institutions provide the oversight.

Verification Model
Stable schema + stable byte representation across environments. Independent reviewers can recompute digests without interpretive variance.
Hashing + digital signature establish tamper-evidence and origin authenticity from the moment of sealing forward.
Versioned capture policy defines inclusion, exclusion, masking, and disclosure tiering prior to generation. Artifacts reflect declared scope; they do not implicitly expand beyond it.
Verification without default exposure of weights, proprietary architecture, or trade-secret implementation details.
[System posture]

A Decoupled Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary — sidecar, gateway, or wrapper — and mirrors only policy-scoped fields for artifact generation. It canonicalizes those fields deterministically and seals artifacts for later verification under declared custody and disclosure posture.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output, legal sufficiency, or compliance outcomes.

01 · Attach
Co-locate or proxy beside inference. No mutation of request/response payloads.
02 · Capture
Policy-scoped inclusion/exclusion/masking. Out-of-scope fields are dropped.
03 · Canonicalize
Deterministic normalization (RFC 8785 JCS) into a stable byte stream.
04 · Seal
SHA-256 digest + ECDSA P-256 signature under non-custodial key custody.
05 · Disclose
Tiered disclosure enables verification without default exposure of proprietary internals.
06 · Verify
Recompute canonical hash, validate signature, confirm policy version and policy hash alignment.

Boundary Declaration · V2026.02.19
CEYO produces integrity-sealed records to support oversight processes. CEYO does not certify compliance, determine legality, validate decision correctness, guarantee inference reproducibility, or guarantee legal admissibility. Oversight conclusions are made by authorized institutions and operators.
Verification posture
Successful verification confirms: (1) the artifact has not been modified since sealing, (2) it originated from the declared source under the declared key custody framework, (3) the capture was governed by the recorded policy version and policy hash. It does not confirm inference correctness or outcome proportionality.
