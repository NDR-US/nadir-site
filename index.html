The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput, latency, and availability. Accountability infrastructure is frequently secondary.

When a decision is later questioned — through regulatory inquiry, internal audit, incident review, or litigation — organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs and distributed traces. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

Institutions require artifacts that are canonical in structure, explicit in capture scope, cryptographically sealed, and independently verifiable. This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

Core Principle
Evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority.
CEYO provides the artifact. Institutions provide the oversight.
01 / Canonical Deterministic structure
Stable schema and stable bytes across heterogeneous environments. Canonicalization ensures logically equivalent structures produce identical byte representations for independent recomputation.
02 / Integrity Cryptographic sealing
After canonicalization, hashing and digital signatures establish tamper-evidence and origin authenticity. Artifacts become independently verifiable records.
03 / Scope Policy-scoped capture
Versioned policy defines inclusion, exclusion, masking, and tiered disclosure before capture. Artifacts reflect declared scope; they do not implicitly expand beyond it.
04 / Separation Oversight without exposure
Constrained disclosure enables integrity validation without forced exposure of proprietary internals. Verification is mechanical; interpretation remains institutional.
System Architecture

A Decoupled Evidentiary Substrate

CEYO attaches at the inference boundary — as a sidecar, wrapper, or gateway — and captures only what operator policy explicitly defines. It normalizes captured fields into a stable artifact schema, applies deterministic canonicalization, and seals cryptographically under a non-custodial key architecture.

Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm correctness of model output.

NOT Records are not judgments
CEYO does not determine legality, fairness, correctness, or compliance outcomes. It produces integrity-sealed records for institutional review.
PRINCIPLE Structural independence
Evidence infrastructure must remain separate from decision authority. CEYO provides artifacts; institutions determine their meaning.
Substrate Properties

Technical Core

01 · Capture
Policy-scoped
Operators define included, excluded, and masked fields before artifact generation begins. Capture policy is explicit and versioned. Policy ID and version are embedded in the artifact.
02 · Structure
Canonical
Captured context is normalized into a deterministic artifact schema. Canonicalization (RFC 8785) produces stable bytes for independent recomputation without interpretive variance.
03 · Sealing
Integrity sealing
After canonicalization, hashing and digital signatures are applied as reference implementations. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence; signatures establish origin authenticity.
04 · Keys
Non-custodial
Signing keys remain within the operator-controlled environment — HSM, KMS, or TEE. CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys.
05 · Disclosure
Constrained
Artifacts may be disclosed under Public/Redacted or Controlled Review tiers. Tiering supports verification without default exposure of model weights or trade-secret internals.
06 · Availability
Fail-open
Inference continuity remains independent of artifact sealing. If sealing fails, inference proceeds. Failures are logged and artifacts are marked unsealed and reviewable.
