Evidentiary Infrastructure

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, infrastructure often produces telemetry—logs and traces never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

CEYO is a decoupled evidentiary layer that attaches beside inference systems to produce policy-scoped, canonical artifacts. These artifacts are cryptographically sealed to support later institutional verification.

// BOUNDARY

CEYO does not evaluate outputs. CEYO produces records.

// DISCLOSURE

Tiered disclosure enables validation without exposure of proprietary internals.

// AVAILABILITY

Fail-open posture preserves inference continuity unless configured otherwise.

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput and latency. When a decision is questioned, organizations reconstruct events from partial logs. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.

This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.
