Telemetry

CEYO is a weight-neutral integrity layer. It captures canonical artifacts of inference-time context, cryptographically sealed for institutional verification.

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI optimizes for throughput. When a decision is questioned, organizations reconstruct events from logs. This is interpretive, not deterministic.

CEYO closes this architectural deficit by producing artifacts that are canonical, explicit in scope, and independently verifiable.

01 / STRUCTURAL Canonical Artifacts
Consistent schema and stable byte representation across environments. CEYO ensures the data looks exactly the same regardless of where it was captured.
02 / CRYPTO Integrity Sealing
SHA-256 hashing and ECDSA digital signatures establish tamper-evidence. Once sealed, an artifact cannot be modified without breaking the chain of trust.
Substrate Properties

Technical Core

01 · CAPTURE
Policy-Scoped
Operators define what fields are included before generation begins. Artifacts reflect declared policy scope; they do not implicitly expand beyond it.
02 · KEYS
Non-Custodial
Signing keys remain in client-controlled HSM/KMS. CEYO never sees your private keys, preserving institution-led cryptographic authority.
03 · FAIL-OPEN
Fail-Open Arch
Inference continuity is independent of artifact sealing. If generation fails, inference proceeds uninterrupted to preserve system availability.
Boundary Declaration v2026

CEYO produces records. It does not validate correctness, fairness, or legal outcomes. Authorized institutions retain full responsibility for determinations.

Verification confirms integrity under policy scope—not the inherent correctness of the AI model output.
