Evidentiary
Infrastructure

Bridging the structural gap between raw telemetry and durable proof through decoupled, cryptographically sealed inference artifacts.

AI systems increasingly influence decisions in regulated, safety-critical, and institutional environments. When those decisions are reviewed, current infrastructure produces telemetry that was never designed to function as forensic evidence.

Telemetry describes activity.
It does not preserve proof.

NADIR attaches to the inference boundary to produce policy-scoped, canonical artifacts. These represent defined inference-time decision-state context, cryptographically sealed for institutional verification.

System_Characteristics

  • Latency < 1.5ms avg

The Accountability Gap

Modern AI deployments optimize for throughput. Accountability is frequently secondary. When a decision is questioned, organizations are forced to reconstruct events from partial, mutable logs.

Institutional Position

NADIR is infrastructure. It is not adjudication. Authorized institutions and regulated operators retain responsibility for determinations. NADIR provides the record; institutions provide the oversight.
