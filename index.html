Evidentiary Infrastructure

AI systems influence high-stakes decisions. Current infrastructure produces logs never designed to function as durable evidence.

Telemetry describes activity. It does not preserve proof.

CEYO attaches beside inference systems to produce policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined decision-state context.

The Accountability Gap

When a decision is questioned, organizations often reconstruct events from partial logs. Reconstruction is interpretive. It is not deterministic.
Institutions require artifacts that are:
• Canonical in structure
• Explicit in capture scope
• Cryptographically sealed
• Independently verifiable

This is an architectural deficit, not an operational oversight.

Institutional Position

CEYO introduces an evidentiary substrate that operates beside inference engines. It captures only what policy defines, normalizes that context, and seals the artifact for tamper-evidence.
CEYO does not evaluate outputs. CEYO does not adjudicate compliance.
CEYO produces records.
Authorized institutions and regulated operators retain responsibility for determinations. CEYO provides the record; institutions provide oversight.
