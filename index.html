Forensic Compliance Layer • Deterministic Artifacts • Constrained Disclosure

Deterministic audit artifacts for AI decisions

NADIR produces a reproducible, tamper-evident record of AI decision-state evolution for oversight and post-incident reconstruction—without exposing weights, source code, or proprietary internals.

How it works Operational integration

What it is

A compliance-grade recording layer that attaches at the inference boundary and outputs verifiable artifacts.

Deterministic capture

Canonicalizes inputs, context, and key runtime signals under a defined determinism boundary.

Tamper-evident signature

Generates a cryptographic fingerprint over an abstracted representation of the decision-state.

Audit-ready record

Persists an artifact designed for chain-of-custody workflows and independent verification.

How it works (high level)

Structured capture → deterministic abstraction (DWSH) → signature → retention record.

1) Capture boundary

Define what is captured at inference time and what is explicitly excluded to preserve confidentiality.

2) Canonicalization

Normalize ordering, rounding, serialization, and metadata to eliminate accidental entropy.

3) DWSH abstraction

Transform internal state into a reduced abstracted representation via deterministic weighting.

4) Sign + record

Generate a tamper-evident fingerprint and store an artifact suitable for verification and oversight.

Reality-bound claim: NADIR verifies integrity and reproducibility (under defined conditions). It does not claim decision correctness and does not provide interpretability-by-disclosure.

What it does not do

Clear boundaries prevent over-claiming and match real oversight expectations.

No weight exposure

Verification without disclosing model weights, architecture, or source code.

No classified dependency

Does not require classified datasets, restricted systems, or government facilities.

No safety replacement

Does not replace safety engineering, policy gating, or human authorization controls.
