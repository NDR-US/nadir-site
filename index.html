NADIR: evidentiary infrastructure for AI inference systems

NADIR is an attachable forensic compliance layer designed to generate policy-scoped, canonical artifacts representing defined inference-time decision-state context, sealed with integrity controls to support later verification and structured oversight.

How it works Document suite Governance posture

The oversight gap

AI inference systems are optimized for performance and scale. Many deployments retain outputs and surface telemetry, but do not preserve canonical artifacts suitable for later integrity verification and reconstruction under formal review. This creates a structural gap between deployment and institutional accountability.

Post-incident reconstruction

Logs often capture what happened at the surface, not what can be verified under defined evidentiary boundaries.

Oversight under constrained disclosure

Institutions need reviewability without forcing exposure of proprietary architecture, weights, or confidential code.

Liability ambiguity

Without integrity-bound artifacts, later disputes rely on incomplete records and non-reproducible context.

What NADIR is

NADIR attaches beside an inference system and produces artifacts designed for verification. Its purpose is to support oversight workflows conducted by authorized institutions and operators. NADIR is infrastructure — its output is a record, not an adjudication.

Policy-scoped capture

Capture scope is defined by configuration: what is included, what is excluded, and what is masked.

Canonical artifact schema

Inputs and defined runtime context are normalized into a schema intended to support integrity verification under defined capture conditions.

Integrity sealing

Artifacts are hashed and signed to support authenticity validation and tamper detection.

Controlled disclosure

Artifacts are structured with tiered visibility so reviewers can verify integrity without accessing proprietary internals.

NADIR does not validate correctness, fairness, legality, or policy compliance of AI systems. It generates structured artifacts to support oversight processes conducted by authorized institutions.
