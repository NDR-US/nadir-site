The Case for Decoupled Evidence: Solving the Integrity Gap in Autonomous Inference

Published: Feb 2026 Category: Infrastructure Engineering
Abstract: As autonomous systems transition from advisory roles to agentic execution, the requirement for durable, forensic-grade evidence becomes an architectural necessity. Current telemetry-based logging is interpretive and mutable. This paper proposes a decoupled evidentiary substrate that utilizes cryptographic sealing and RFC 8785 canonicalization to create non-repudiable records of decision-state.

I. The Failure of Observability

Observability tools—designed for latency monitoring and error tracing—are fundamentally unsuited for legal or regulatory review. Because observability data is often sampled, mutable, and stored in vendor-specific formats, it lacks the evidentiary density required for institutional accountability.

II. The Decoupling Principle

For an evidence record to be trusted, it must be independent of the system it describes. By attaching NADIR at the inference boundary, we create a "Flight Recorder" effect. The record is created in real-time but exists as a standalone cryptographic artifact that survives the session.

"We do not need more logs. We need more proof."

— NADIR Systems Engineering

III. Conclusion

The future of autonomous infrastructure depends on the ability of human institutions to verify machine intent. Without a decoupled integrity layer, AI systems remain "black boxes" not because of their complexity, but because of their lack of formal record-keeping.
