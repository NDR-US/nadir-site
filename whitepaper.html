The Case for Decoupled Evidence: Solving the Integrity Gap.

Published: Feb 2026 Substrate: NADIR v1.0 Ref: 0944-X
Note 01: On the necessity of forensic-grade artifacts in agentic AI.
Abstract: As autonomous systems transition from advisory roles to agentic execution, the requirement for durable evidence becomes an architectural necessity. Telemetry is interpretive; NADIR provides deterministic decision-state context through cryptographic sealing.

I. The Failure of Observability

Observability tools—designed for latency monitoring and error tracing—are fundamentally unsuited for legal or regulatory review. Because observability data is often sampled, mutable, and stored in vendor-specific formats, it lacks the evidentiary density required for institutional accountability.

In the event of a high-stakes failure, traditional logs provide a "guess" at what happened. NADIR provides a "seal" of what happened.

"We do not need more logs. We need more proof."

II. The Decoupling Principle

For an evidence record to be trusted, it must be independent of the system it describes. By attaching NADIR at the inference boundary, we create a **"Flight Recorder"** effect. The record is created in real-time but exists as a standalone cryptographic artifact that survives the session, independent of the model's state.

III. Conclusion

The future of autonomous infrastructure depends on the ability of human institutions to verify machine intent. Without a decoupled integrity layer, AI systems remain "black boxes" not because of their complexity, but because of their lack of formal, verifiable record-keeping.
