Vulnerability disclosure program

NADIR encourages responsible disclosure of security vulnerabilities affecting publicly accessible NADIR-owned assets. This program provides a reporting channel and coordinated response posture. It is not a bug bounty program and does not guarantee financial compensation.

Report a vulnerability Trust center
[REPORTING]

How to report

If you believe you have identified a security vulnerability in a NADIR-controlled public asset, please report it through the channel below. Do not publicly disclose the issue until coordinated remediation has occurred.

[SECURITY CONTACT]

Email: security@nadir-ai.com

Please include:

  • A clear description of the vulnerability and affected asset.
  • Steps to reproduce (if applicable).
  • Proof-of-concept details sufficient for validation.
  • Your contact information for coordinated follow-up.

Encrypted submissions may be supported upon request. Public PGP keys, if available, will be published on this page.

[SCOPE]

In-scope and out-of-scope

The VDP applies to publicly accessible NADIR-owned domains and assets. It does not apply to third-party integrations, client-controlled deployments, or infrastructure outside NADIR’s direct control.

[IN SCOPE]
  • NADIR-owned public web domains.
  • Public documentation assets hosted by NADIR.
  • Security misconfigurations directly attributable to NADIR-controlled infrastructure.
[OUT OF SCOPE]
  • Client deployments or environments.
  • Social engineering or physical attacks.
  • Denial-of-service testing without authorization.
  • Issues requiring unauthorized data access.

Testing must not degrade service availability, violate privacy, or access data beyond what is strictly necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability. NADIR reserves the right to determine scope applicability.

[COORDINATED DISCLOSURE]

Response timeline

NADIR will acknowledge receipt of valid vulnerability reports within a reasonable timeframe and will coordinate remediation and disclosure in good faith.

[ACKNOWLEDGMENT]

Initial acknowledgment within a commercially reasonable timeframe.

[VALIDATION]

Internal validation and risk assessment. Clarifications may be requested from the reporter.

[REMEDIATION]

Remediation planning and deployment, followed by coordinated disclosure where appropriate.

NADIR may publish security advisories where remediation materially affects publicly exposed systems.

[SAFE HARBOR]

Good-faith research protection

NADIR will not pursue legal action against individuals who act in good faith and in accordance with this policy.

If you conduct testing consistent with this policy, avoid privacy violations, avoid service disruption, and provide timely disclosure through the reporting channel, NADIR considers your research authorized for the limited purpose of vulnerability identification and coordinated disclosure.

This safe harbor does not extend to actions outside the scope defined above or to violations of law.
