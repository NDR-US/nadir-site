Vulnerability Disclosure Program.

CEYO encourages responsible reporting of security vulnerabilities affecting CEYO-controlled systems. This policy describes how to report issues, what is in scope, and how coordination is handled.

Good faith
No disruption
Coordinated disclosure
Version

V2026.02.19 · San Diego, California

1. Purpose

CEYO encourages responsible reporting of security vulnerabilities. Reports help improve the safety and reliability of CEYO’s public website and CEYO-controlled systems.

2. Reporting

Send reports to: contact@ceyo.ai

Include
  • Description of the issue and expected impact.
  • Reproduction steps and any relevant request/response samples.
  • Affected pages/endpoints/components (if known).
  • Proof-of-concept (only if it does not exfiltrate data or disrupt service).

3. Safe harbor

CEYO will not pursue legal action for good-faith security research that avoids privacy violations, service disruption, and data exfiltration, and that complies with this policy.

Good-faith boundaries
  • Test only within scope and only with accounts/data you own.
  • Do not access, modify, delete, or exfiltrate data.
  • Stop once the issue is confirmed. Do not chain exploits or establish persistence.
  • Do not use social engineering, phishing, or physical security testing.

4. Scope

  • CEYO public website and CEYO-controlled systems only.
  • Do not test third-party systems or services.
Out of scope
  • Denial of service (DoS), load/stress testing, or traffic flooding.
  • Credential stuffing, brute force, or testing accounts you do not own.
  • Accessing, modifying, deleting, or exfiltrating any data.
  • Testing vendors, hosting providers, or any infrastructure not controlled by CEYO.
  • Physical security, threats, or social engineering (phishing/vishing).

5. Coordination

Allow reasonable time to investigate and remediate prior to public disclosure. CEYO will coordinate in good faith and may request clarification or additional reproduction detail.

6. Response timelines

  • Acknowledgment: within 3 business days.
  • Initial assessment: within 10 business days.
  • Status updates: at least every 14 days until closure or resolution.

Timelines may vary based on severity and complexity. If an issue is actively exploited, CEYO may prioritize remediation over public communications.

7. Disclosure & credit

  • Coordinated disclosure is expected.
  • Public disclosure is welcome after a fix is deployed or an agreed timeline is reached.
  • Researcher credit (name/handle) may be provided upon request; anonymous reporting is accepted.

8. Boundary declaration

This policy does not authorize unlawful access or activity. If you are unsure whether a test is in scope, contact CEYO before proceeding.

Contact

Reports and questions: contact@ceyo.ai
