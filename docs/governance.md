# CEYO Governance and Use Policy

## Purpose

CEYO is designed to support transparency, accountability, and verifiable oversight for AI-driven systems.

The system generates cryptographically verifiable artifacts that allow independent parties to validate the integrity of AI system events.

This document describes the governance principles and intended usage boundaries for CEYO.

---

## Governance Principles

### Transparency

CEYO artifacts provide structured records of AI system events that can be independently verified.

These records help reduce ambiguity during incident investigation, regulatory review, or system auditing.

---

### Independent Verification

Artifacts generated by CEYO can be verified without access to the original AI system or proprietary model details.

Verification relies on deterministic canonicalization and cryptographic signatures.

---

### Policy-Scoped Data Capture

CEYO artifacts record only explicitly defined fields.

Capture policies determine:

- which fields are included
- which fields are excluded
- which fields are masked or hashed

This ensures artifacts remain privacy-conscious and limited to relevant oversight data.

---

### Model Neutrality

CEYO does not modify, instrument, or interfere with the internal operation of AI models.

The system operates as an external evidentiary layer.

---

### Institutional Oversight

CEYO artifacts are intended to support oversight performed by:

- regulators
- auditors
- investigators
- system operators
- independent review bodies

CEYO does not perform oversight itself.

---

## Acceptable Use

CEYO is intended for use in environments where verifiable records of AI system events are required.

Examples include:

- autonomous systems
- AI-assisted decision support
- safety-critical automation
- regulatory compliance contexts
- incident investigation workflows

---

## Prohibited Interpretations

Artifacts generated by CEYO should not be interpreted as:

- proof of legal compliance
- certification of AI safety
- confirmation of decision correctness
- guarantees of model fairness
- regulatory approval

Artifacts represent records, not judgments.

---

## Data Responsibility

Operators deploying CEYO are responsible for:

- defining artifact capture policies
- protecting cryptographic signing keys
- ensuring artifact storage integrity
- complying with applicable privacy laws

CEYO does not manage operator cryptographic keys.

---

## Human Rights Considerations

Deployments involving AI systems should respect internationally recognized human rights principles.

Operators are responsible for ensuring that CEYO deployments do not facilitate unlawful surveillance, discrimination, or rights violations.

---

## Scope of Responsibility

CEYO provides infrastructure for generating verifiable records.

Responsibility for interpretation, investigation, and enforcement remains with relevant institutions and authorities.

---

## Revision

This governance policy may evolve as the CEYO project develops.

Updates will be reflected through versioned documentation.
