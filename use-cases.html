OVERLAY ATTACHMENT · CHAIN-OF-CUSTODY POSTURE · CONSTRAINED DISCLOSURE

Operational integration

NADIR is an attachable integrity overlay. It does not replace inference systems, assume operational control, or modify core model parameters. Implementation architecture is under active development.

Architecture mechanics Roadmap Documents

Integration posture

Attach at the boundary

Sidecar, gateway, or wrapper around inference calls. Avoids core model rewrite.

Policy-defined scope

Capture fields are configured per deployment; unnecessary collection is explicitly excluded.

Chain-of-custody posture

Artifacts are structured to support retention, verification, and review under oversight processes.

Deployment contexts (illustrative)

Use cases are descriptive and do not imply endorsement, certification, or operational authorization.

Regulated decision-support systems

Integrity-sealed records for structured oversight and accountability review.

Autonomous navigation record preservation

Artifacts for route selection, escalation, and failover behavior under defined capture policy.

Incident reconstruction workflows

Artifacts structured to support chain-of-custody workflows for review boards where disclosure must remain constrained.

Institutional oversight review environments

Integrity-sealed records for prioritization, triage, escalation, and analyst-assist workflows under constrained disclosure.

Boundary declaration
  • NADIR supports integrity verification and forensic reconstruction workflows.
  • NADIR does not replace safety engineering, policy gating, or human authorization controls.
  • NADIR does not assume operational control over inference infrastructure.
  • Deployment responsibility remains with the implementing organization.
