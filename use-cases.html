Operational integration

NADIR is an attachable integrity overlay. It does not replace inference systems, assume operational control, or modify core model parameters. Implementation architecture is under active development.

View posture Roadmap
[POSTURE]

Attachable integrity layer

Integration is intended to be low-friction. Capture scope is defined by policy, and unnecessary collection is explicitly excluded.

[BOUNDARY ATTACH]

Sidecar, gateway middleware, or wrapper around inference calls. Instrumentation outside core model logic and weights.

[SCOPE CONTROL]

Policy-defined field selection, masking/redaction rules, disclosure tier definitions, and versioning requirements.

[CHAIN-OF-CUSTODY]

Artifacts are structured for retention, verification, and review under oversight processes.

[DEPLOYMENT CONTEXTS]
  • Regulated decision-support systems: integrity-sealed records for structured oversight review.
  • Autonomous navigation record preservation: route selection, escalation, and failover behavior under defined capture policy.
  • Incident reconstruction: artifacts structured for review boards where disclosure must remain constrained.
  • Institutional oversight environments: prioritization, triage, and analyst-assist workflows under tiered disclosure.

Use cases are descriptive only and do not imply endorsement, certification, or operational authorization.

[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR supports integrity verification and forensic reconstruction workflows.
  • NADIR does not replace safety engineering, policy gating, or human authorization controls.
  • Deployment responsibility remains with the implementing organization.
