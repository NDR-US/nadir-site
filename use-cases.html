Operational integration

NADIR is designed to attach at the inference-call boundary (gateway, middleware, or wrapper service) to produce audit artifacts with minimal disruption to existing deployments.

Architecture Roadmap

Integration posture

NADIR attaches at an interface where inputs are received and inference outputs are produced. This enables phased adoption: instrument first, expand scope only after determinism boundaries are proven.

Governance note

NADIR artifacts support integrity validation and reconstruction. They do not substitute for safety engineering, human authorization, or rules-of-engagement governance.

Audit artifacts are strongest when paired with policy gating, logging discipline, and retention controls.

Representative use cases

Examples are framed conservatively to avoid implying capabilities beyond audit artifacts.

Autonomous navigation

Artifacts supporting review of route selection, failover behavior, and decision-state divergence during incidents.

Decision support systems

Integrity-anchored artifacts supporting post-event review of prioritization, escalation, and operator-assist decisions.

Validation gates

Deterministic signatures used as part of governance workflows prior to downstream actions in regulated contexts.

Incident reconstruction

Chain-of-custody posture supporting after-action review under constrained disclosure requirements.
