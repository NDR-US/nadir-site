Operational integration

NADIR is designed to attach at the inference boundary (gateway, middleware, or service wrapper) to produce integrity-sealed artifacts without modifying core model parameters. Deployment can be phased, and disclosure can be tiered.

Architecture mechanics Roadmap Documents

Integration posture

NADIR is not a platform replacement. It is an attachable integrity layer that produces auditable records for later verification. Integration is intended to be low-friction and does not require exposing proprietary internals to begin generating artifacts. Implementation architecture is under active development.

Attach at the boundary

Sidecar, gateway, or wrapper around inference calls—avoids core model rewrite.

Policy-defined scope

Capture fields are configured per deployment; unnecessary collection is explicitly excluded.

Chain-of-custody fit

Artifacts are structured for retention, verification, and review under oversight processes.

Primary use cases

Use cases are framed conservatively: NADIR supports integrity and reconstruction workflows. It does not certify correctness or safety.

Autonomous navigation systems

Artifacts for route selection, obstacle response, escalation, and failover behavior under defined capture policy.

ISR decision support

Integrity-sealed records for prioritization, triage, escalation, and analyst-assist workflows under constrained disclosure.

AI-enabled targeting validation

Artifacts to support review gates and accountability procedures—without disclosing model internals on the public surface.

Incident reconstruction

Chain-of-custody-ready artifacts for review boards where disclosure must remain constrained.

Boundary declaration
  • NADIR supports integrity verification and forensic reconstruction workflows.
  • NADIR does not replace safety engineering, policy gating, or human authorization controls.
  • NADIR does not assume operational control over inference infrastructure.
  • Deployment responsibility remains with the implementing organization.
