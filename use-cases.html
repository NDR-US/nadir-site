Operational Integration

NADIR is designed to attach at the inference boundary (gateway, middleware, or service wrapper) to generate policy-scoped, integrity-sealed artifacts without modifying model weights. Deployment can be phased, disclosure can be tiered, and verification can be performed by authorized reviewers under defined access controls.

Enter integration Platform
[INTEGRATION POSTURE]

Attachable layer, not a platform replacement

Integration is designed to be low-friction and weight-neutral: NADIR can begin producing artifacts without requiring disclosure of proprietary architecture or training data. Implementation architecture may evolve across phases; public claims remain bounded to verifiable record-generation and integrity controls.

[BOUNDARY]

Attach at inference edge

Sidecar, gateway, or wrapper around inference calls to avoid core model rewrite and reduce adoption risk.

[POLICY]

Policy-scoped capture

Capture fields are configured per deployment; unnecessary collection is explicitly excluded. Masking rules and tiered disclosure are part of scope definition.

[CUSTODY]

Review-ready records

Artifacts are structured for retention, retrieval logging, verification, and structured oversight workflows under constrained disclosure.

[FAILURE POSTURE]
  • Fail-open behavior can be used so inference availability is not automatically interrupted by sealing-layer failure.
  • When sealing fails, the event can be logged and flagged for review as unsealed under the applicable policy.
  • Availability requirements and escalation thresholds should be defined by the implementing organization.
[DEPLOYMENT CONTEXTS]

Illustrative use contexts for verifiable records

Use contexts are descriptive and do not imply endorsement, certification, or operational authorization. NADIR’s function is record-generation and integrity verification support under defined boundaries.

[REGULATED OPS]

Regulated decision-support

Integrity-sealed artifacts for structured oversight and accountability review where decisions are later disputed or audited.

[AUTONOMY]

Autonomous system traceability

Artifacts capturing route selection, escalation, and failover behavior under defined capture policy to support reconstruction workflows.

[INCIDENTS]

Incident reconstruction

Chain-of-custody-oriented artifacts for review boards where disclosure must remain constrained and integrity must be independently verified.

[BOUNDARY DECLARATION]
  • NADIR supports integrity verification and forensic reconstruction workflows.
  • NADIR does not replace safety engineering, policy gating, or human authorization controls.
  • NADIR does not assume operational control over inference infrastructure.
  • Deployment responsibility remains with the implementing organization.
[CONSTRAINED DISCLOSURE]

Tiered visibility for review without IP exposure

Disclosure boundaries are policy-defined and can vary by audience and agreement. Reviewers can validate integrity and policy alignment without default access to proprietary internals.

[TIER 01]

Public / redacted

High-level artifact descriptors and non-sensitive metadata suitable for public-facing posture and general transparency.

[TIER 02]

Controlled review

Expanded fields necessary for oversight review under authorization, with masking rules enforced by policy scope.

[TIER 03]

Operator internal

Deployment-specific fields and internal operational context intended for regulated operators under their own governance controls.
