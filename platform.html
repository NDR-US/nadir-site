Architecture

NADIR follows a strict mechanics chain: attach at the inference boundary, generate a canonical artifact schema, seal it with integrity controls, enforce controlled disclosure, and enable independent verification.

Operational integration Document suite

1) Capture model

NADIR is designed to attach non-intrusively at the inference boundary (sidecar, gateway, or wrapper). Capture scope is policy-defined and explicitly bounded to avoid unnecessary collection and to preserve confidential implementation boundaries.

Inference boundary attachment

Instrumentation point is outside core model logic to reduce adoption friction and avoid mutating weights.

Policy-scoped capture

Capture includes defined inputs and runtime context; excludes proprietary weights, architecture details, and unrelated data.

2) Artifact schema

Captured context is normalized into a canonical artifact structure intended to support reproducible verification under defined conditions. The schema is designed for audit workflows and chain-of-custody compatible storage.

Canonical artifact fields (example)

Timestamp · Provenance metadata · Input hash · Policy ID · Canonicalization version · Disclosure tier · Integrity fields · Verification fields

3) Integrity model

Artifacts are sealed using cryptographic hashing and digital signatures to support authenticity validation and tamper detection. Specific primitives can be selected per deployment requirements.

Hashing

Produces a fixed digest of the canonical artifact to detect modification.

Digital signature

Authenticates artifact origin under key custody and verification policies.

Time anchoring

Supports evidentiary posture by binding artifact integrity to reliable timestamps and retention controls.

4) Disclosure field model

NADIR supports tiered visibility: reviewers verify the fingerprint and integrity fields without receiving proprietary internals. Disclosure boundaries are policy-defined and can vary by audience and agreement.

Public / redacted

High-level description of artifacts and controls without sensitive operational detail.

Controlled review

Authorized reviewers receive expanded fields necessary for oversight while preserving trade secret boundaries.

5) Verification workflow

Verification is performed by recomputing hashes over the canonical artifact, validating signatures, and confirming policy alignment. The goal is to confirm integrity and provenance—not to certify decision correctness.

Boundary Declaration
  • NADIR verifies artifact integrity and provenance under policy.
  • NADIR does not certify compliance or model correctness.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized institutions and operators.
