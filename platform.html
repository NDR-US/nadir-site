DECOUPLED VERIFICATION ARCHITECTURE · INFERENCE-BOUNDARY ATTACHMENT

Verification Substrate
At the Inference Boundary

CEYO operates beside AI inference systems as a weight-neutral evidentiary layer. It captures policy-scoped inference-time context, normalizes into canonical artifacts, applies deterministic canonicalization (RFC 8785), and seals artifacts cryptographically for later verification under constrained disclosure.

Policy-Scoped Capture Define before you collect
High-level
Artifact generation begins with explicit policy definition. Only fields declared within policy scope are captured. Policy identifiers and versions are embedded within artifacts to make boundaries reviewable.
Institutional-grade detail
  • Policy defines inclusion/exclusion, masking/redaction rules, and disclosure tiers prior to generation.
  • Policy ID + version recorded inside the artifact; artifacts are rejected as “Policy Scope Mismatch” if misaligned.
  • Capture minimization is structural (pre-generation), not improvised post hoc.
RFC 8785 Canonicalization Deterministic bytes
High-level
Before hashing and signing, JSON artifacts are canonicalized using RFC 8785 to ensure logically equivalent structures yield identical byte output across environments.
Institutional-grade detail
  • Canonicalization is a prerequisite for reproducible hashing across runtimes and serializers.
  • Verification recomputes the canonical bytes via RFC 8785 and compares derived digest + signature validity.
  • Canonical form reduces interpretive variance in post-incident reconstruction workflows.
Cryptographic Sealing Hash + signature
High-level
After canonicalization, artifacts are sealed using hashing and digital signatures. Reference suite: SHA-256 and ECDSA (P-256).
Institutional-grade detail
  • Digest computed over canonical bytes; signature computed over digest (or canonical bytes per suite contract).
  • Verification requires operator public key provenance and declared suite identifiers.
  • Suite selection remains configurable to meet institutional requirements; claims remain proportional to executed suite.
Independent Verification Deterministic workflow
High-level
Verification includes RFC 8785 canonicalization, hash recomputation, signature validation, and policy identifier/version alignment checks.
Institutional-grade detail
  • Outcome states: Integrity Confirmed, Integrity Failed, Policy Scope Mismatch.
  • Verification confirms integrity + provenance under declared scope, not correctness of inference output.
  • Audit workflows can be executed independently without proprietary model weights by default.
Constrained Disclosure Validation without exposure
High-level
Artifacts support tiered disclosure so integrity can be verified without default exposure of proprietary internals.
Institutional-grade detail
  • Policy defines masking rules and disclosure tiers prior to generation.
  • Disclosure tier is embedded to prevent ambiguity about what was included or intentionally excluded.
  • Controlled review can expand scope only under written agreement and revised policy versioning.
Availability Posture Fail-open by design
High-level
Sealing failures may be logged and flagged without interrupting inference unless explicitly configured otherwise.
Institutional-grade detail
  • Availability remains an institutional decision (fail-open vs fail-closed by explicit configuration).
  • Unsealed or partially sealed records remain reviewable as “integrity not confirmed,” preserving transparency.
  • Operational metrics and overhead are deployment-dependent; no universal latency claims are made.
