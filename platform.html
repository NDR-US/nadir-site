Platform mechanics chain

NADIR follows a strict mechanics chain: attach at the inference boundary, capture policy-scoped fields, normalize into a canonical artifact, canonicalize deterministically (RFC 8785 for JSON artifacts), hash and sign, enforce tiered disclosure, and enable independent verification.

View stages Technical documents
[STAGES]

Core stages (Phase 1)

Adoption posture is non-intrusive. Instrumentation sits outside core weights and inference logic to reduce friction and preserve implementation boundaries.

[STAGE 1]

Capture model

Attach at the inference boundary (sidecar, gateway, or wrapper). Capture scope is policy-defined: included fields, excluded fields, masking rules, tier, version.

[STAGE 2]

Canonical schema

Normalize captured context into a canonical artifact structure designed for audit workflows and chain-of-custody compatible storage.

[STAGE 3]

Integrity sealing

Canonicalize deterministically (RFC 8785), hash, and digitally sign. Cryptographic suites are deployment-defined and may evolve.

[STAGE 4]

Disclosure tiers

Tiered visibility enables integrity verification without default exposure of weights, confidential architecture, or trade secrets.

[STAGE 5]

Verification workflow

Recompute hashes, validate signatures, and confirm policy alignment. Verification confirms integrity and provenance, not correctness or legality.

[FAIL-OPEN]

Availability posture

Mandatory fail-open: if sealing fails, inference continues, the event is logged, and the artifact is flagged unsealed for review.

[OPERATIONAL SCRUTINY]
  • Resource isolation: enforce cgroup-based quotas so the sidecar cannot create back-pressure on inference compute.
  • Non-custodial keys: signing keys are generated and retained in the client-controlled environment (HSM/KMS); NADIR does not possess private keys.
  • Verification determinism: RFC 8785 canonicalization prior to hashing for consistent integrity across heterogeneous environments.
[BOUNDARY]
  • NADIR does not certify compliance, legality, or correctness.
  • NADIR does not guarantee inference reproducibility or semantic equivalence of outputs.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized institutions and operators.
