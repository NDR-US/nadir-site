Platform & Architecture

Decoupled Verification
Architecture

CEYO is designed as a verification substrate that operates beside AI inference systems without modifying model weights or core decision logic. Its purpose is narrow: to generate deterministic, integrity-sealed artifacts representing defined inference-time context under policy scope.

[01] Attachment at the Inference Boundary

Attach Beside
Inference

CEYO integrates at the inference boundary through a sidecar, gateway, or API wrapper configuration. This posture is intentional. Evidence infrastructure should not require mutation of model weights, training pipelines, or core inference engines.

Attachment at the boundary allows CEYO to observe defined inference events, capture policy-scoped context, normalize event structure, produce deterministic artifacts, and seal artifacts without interfering with decision flow.

Mode
Sidecar
Runs alongside the inference service; observes defined events; isolates sealing workload.
Mode
Gateway
Intercepts requests at an edge boundary; enforces policy scope and tier selection.
Mode
API Wrapper
Wraps inference calls in application code; low friction for incremental adoption.
Principle
Non-mutating
Inference engine remains unchanged; integrity layer operates externally.

01 · Policy-Scoped Capture
Define Scope Before Generation
Artifact generation begins with explicit policy definition. The deploying institution defines inclusion, exclusion, masking rules, disclosure tier configuration, and policy version identifiers. Only fields declared within policy scope are captured.
02 · Canonical Artifact Structure
Normalize Into Stable Records
Captured context is normalized into a canonical artifact schema. Artifacts include structured metadata such as timestamp, policy identifier and version, capture environment fingerprint, disclosure tier designation, canonicalization version, integrity fields, and verification metadata where applicable.
03 · Deterministic Canonicalization
RFC 8785 JSON Canonicalization
Before hashing, JSON artifacts are canonicalized using RFC 8785 to ensure logically equivalent structures produce identical byte representations across environments. This enables reproducible hashing and independent verification.
04 · Cryptographic Sealing
Hash + Digital Signature
After canonicalization, artifacts are sealed using hashing and digital signatures under a deployment-defined cryptographic suite. Hashing establishes tamper-evidence. Signatures establish origin authenticity. Reference implementations may include SHA-256 and ECDSA (P-256).
05 · Non-Custodial Key Architecture
Operator-Controlled Signing Keys
Signing keys are generated and retained within the client-controlled environment (HSM, KMS, or TEE). CEYO does not possess, transmit, or store private signing keys. Authenticity remains anchored to the deploying institution.
06 · Time Anchoring (Optional)
Strengthen Contemporaneousness Claims
Deployments may optionally integrate trusted timestamp authorities (RFC 3161), secure time sources, or cryptographic time attestation. Time anchoring is deployment-defined and not mandatory for artifact generation.
07 · Disclosure Tier Enforcement
Verify Without Forced Exposure
Artifacts support tiered disclosure. Public or redacted tiers may expose limited metadata. Controlled review tiers may expose expanded context aligned to authorized oversight requirements. Disclosure boundaries are policy-defined before generation.
08 · Integrity–Availability Balance
Fail-Open Availability Posture
If artifact sealing fails, inference may continue uninterrupted; failures are logged, artifacts are marked unsealed, and conditions remain reviewable. Operators may configure stricter posture when required by policy.
[03] Independent Verification Workflow

Mechanical Verification
Outcomes

Verification is designed to be executable by independent parties using deterministic procedures. Verification confirms integrity and provenance under declared policy scope. It does not confirm semantic correctness or decision validity.

Step
1. Canonicalize (RFC 8785)
Deterministic canonicalization produces reproducible byte output for hashing.
Step
2. Recompute Hash
Hash mismatch indicates alteration since sealing.
Step
3. Validate Signature
Signature validation confirms origin authenticity against the operator public key.
Step
4. Policy Alignment Check
Confirms policy identifier and version alignment; detects scope mismatch.
Outcome
Integrity Confirmed
Hash and signature validate. No alteration detected under declared scope.
Outcome
Integrity Failed
Hash mismatch or signature failure detected.
Outcome
Policy Scope Mismatch
Artifact policy identifier/version does not match expected configuration.
Boundary
Not Output Validation
Verification is integrity and provenance only, not correctness, fairness, or legality.
[04] Operational Integration

Low-Friction Deployment
Posture

CEYO is designed to integrate without requiring modification of core model weights or inference logic. It operates as an attachable integrity layer at the inference boundary, enabling deterministic artifact generation while preserving system performance and autonomy.

Upon inference event: policy-scoped fields are captured, normalized into canonical schema, canonicalized, hashed, digitally signed, and stored according to operator-defined retention posture. Retention duration and chain-of-custody procedures remain institutional responsibilities.

Boundary Declaration
CEYO does not modify model weights or intervene in decision logic.
CEYO does not certify compliance or guarantee admissibility.
Policy scope is explicit and versioned; capture is minimized by design.
Fail-open availability preserves inference continuity unless configured otherwise.
