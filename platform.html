Technical architecture (the DWSH engine)

The innovation resides in the Differential Weight-State Hashing (DWSH) module, a deterministic compliance-layer module operably coupled to an AI inference engine.

DWSH system configuration

A structured capture → deterministic abstraction → cryptographic signature pipeline designed for audit workflows.

1) Input State Capture

Receives structured inputs to generate a cryptographic hash at time T0.

2) State Transformation

Transforms internal vectors into a reduced abstracted representation via deterministic weighting.

3) NADIR Signature (DWSH)

Generates a tamper-evident cryptographic fingerprint derived from the abstracted representation.

4) Audit Artifact Record

Persists a chain-of-custody-ready artifact for retrieval, verification, and oversight review.

Functional constraints

These constraints are non-negotiable. They keep the system adoption-ready and legally defensible.

Reproducibility

The fingerprint is reproducible under identical conditions (defined determinism boundary).

Non-disclosure

It does not disclose model weights or source code.

Evidentiary posture

It is suitable for evidentiary preservation and regulatory oversight.

Correctness disclaimer

Integrity is verifiable; decision correctness is not claimed by the signature itself.

Determinism boundary: determinism is engineered, not assumed. NADIR requires canonicalization of captured state and documented inference conditions (software/hardware constraints, randomness control, instrumentation stability) to ensure reproducible artifacts.

What NADIR does not do

This prevents over-claiming and aligns with real oversight expectations.

No weight exposure

NADIR does not require revealing model weights or proprietary architecture.

No classified dependency

NADIR does not require classified datasets, restricted systems, or government facilities.

No explainability promise

NADIR is audit artifact infrastructure, not interpretability-by-disclosure.
