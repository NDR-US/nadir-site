Architecture

Following an institutional hardening posture, NADIR uses a strict mechanics chain: (1) attach at the inference boundary, (2) generate a canonical artifact schema, (3) seal with cryptographic integrity controls, (4) enforce controlled disclosure fields, and (5) enable independent verification.

Integration Document suite

1) Capture model

NADIR is designed to attach non-intrusively at the inference boundary (sidecar, gateway, or wrapper). Capture scope is policy-defined: what is included, excluded, and masked is explicit.

Boundary attachment

Instrumentation is outside core model logic to reduce adoption friction and avoid modifying weights.

Policy-scoped capture

Capture is limited to defined fields required for oversight workflows; unnecessary collection is avoided by design.

2) Artifact schema

Captured context is normalized into a canonical artifact. Canonicalization reduces ambiguity and supports repeatable verification under defined conditions.

Canonical artifact (example fields)

Timestamp · Provenance metadata · Policy ID · Disclosure tier · Input hash · Canonicalization version · Runtime context (policy-scoped) · Integrity fields · Verification fields

3) Integrity model

NADIR seals artifacts using hashing and digital signatures to support authenticity validation and tamper detection. Cryptographic primitives may vary by deployment requirements.

Hash

Produces a fixed digest of the canonical artifact to detect modification.

Signature

Authenticates artifact origin under key custody and verification policies.

Time anchoring

Binds integrity posture to reliable timestamps and retention controls suitable for review workflows.

4) Disclosure field model

NADIR is designed for constrained disclosure: auditors verify integrity fields and fingerprints while proprietary details remain masked. Disclosure policy can vary by audience and agreement.

Redacted / public posture

High-level description of controls with sensitive operational detail omitted.

Controlled review posture

Authorized reviewers receive expanded fields necessary for oversight while preserving trade secret boundaries.

5) Verification workflow

Verification is performed by recomputing hashes over the canonical artifact, validating signatures, and confirming policy alignment. The goal is to confirm integrity and provenance—not to certify decision correctness.

Boundary Declaration
  • NADIR supports integrity and provenance verification under defined policy.
  • NADIR does not certify compliance or determine correctness.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized institutions and operators.
