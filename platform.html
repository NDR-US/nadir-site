Technical architecture

NADIR’s core module, Differential Weight-State Hashing (DWSH), generates a deterministic abstracted representation of decision-state and signs it to produce a verifiable audit artifact.

Integration posture Documents

System flow (audit artifact pipeline)

Capture → canonicalize → abstract (DWSH) → sign → record.

Input & context capture

Structured inputs + required runtime context are captured at time T0 inside a defined boundary.

Canonicalization

Deterministic serialization and normalization prevent accidental entropy across runs.

DWSH abstraction

Internal state is transformed into a reduced abstracted representation via deterministic weighting.

Signature + record

A tamper-evident fingerprint is generated and stored with provenance metadata for verification.

Functional constraints

Non-negotiable constraints that keep the system defensible and adoption-ready.

Reproducibility

Artifacts are reproducible under identical conditions inside the determinism boundary.

Constrained disclosure

Verification does not require revealing weights, source code, or proprietary architecture.

Evidentiary posture

Record format supports preservation and review under chain-of-custody expectations.

Correctness boundary

Integrity is verifiable; correctness of the decision is not claimed by the signature.

Determinism boundary: determinism is engineered, not assumed. NADIR requires canonicalization of captured state and documented inference conditions (software/hardware constraints, randomness control, instrumentation stability).

What NADIR does not do

Boundaries that reduce legal risk and prevent over-claiming.

No weight exposure

No requirement to reveal weights or proprietary model internals.

No classified dependency

No requirement for classified datasets, restricted systems, or government facilities.

No interpretability promise

Audit artifact infrastructure, not interpretability-by-disclosure.
