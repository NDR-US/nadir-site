Capture • Schema • Integrity • Disclosure • Verification

How NADIR works

NADIR follows a strict mechanics chain: attach at the inference boundary, generate a canonical record, seal it with cryptographic integrity, enforce controlled disclosure, and enable independent verification.

Mechanics

1) Capture

Non-intrusive attachment at the inference boundary. Capture scope is policy-defined, explicitly bounded, and excludes proprietary weights or confidential architecture by default.

2) Canonical schema

Captured context is normalized into a stable, versioned record format designed to reduce entropy and support repeatable verification under defined conditions.

3) Integrity sealing

Each record is hashed and digitally signed to enable authenticity validation and tamper detection.

4) Controlled disclosure

Tiered visibility allows reviewers to verify integrity and provenance without accessing proprietary model internals.

5) Verification workflow

Independent verification recomputes the hash and validates the signature, confirming integrity and provenance — not decision correctness.

Technical detail

Canonical record fields
The canonical record may include policy identifiers, provenance metadata, timestamps, disclosure tiers, and integrity fields.
timestamp provenance policy_id disclosure_tier canonicalization_version input_hash integrity: { hash, signature, key_id } verification: { verifier_fields }
Integrity primitives
Typical implementations may use SHA-256 for hashing and ECDSA for digital signatures, with key custody defined by operator security policy.
hash = SHA-256(canonical_record) signature = ECDSA_sign(private_key, hash) verify = ECDSA_verify(public_key, hash, signature)
Boundary Declaration
  • NADIR verifies record integrity and provenance under defined policy.
  • NADIR does not certify legality, safety, fairness, or compliance.
  • Oversight conclusions are made by authorized reviewers.
