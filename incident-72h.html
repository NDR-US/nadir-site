72-Hour Incident Playbook

A step-by-step guide for producing a verified incident package from NADIR artifacts under constrained disclosure. This is an operational support document. It does not define legal obligations.

Enter playbook Admissibility primer
[WORKFLOW]

Verified incident package

The objective is a defensible package: timestamps, policy identifiers, integrity verification results, and a minimal narrative summary. Keep scope bounded and disclose only what is authorized.

[STEP 01]

Stabilize scope

Identify the impacted system boundary, time window, and policy scope identifier used for capture.

[STEP 02]

Extract artifacts

Retrieve the relevant artifact set from storage under chain-of-custody procedures defined by the operator.

[STEP 03]

Verify integrity

Canonicalize deterministically, recompute hashes, validate signatures, and record PASS/FAIL results.

[STEP 04]

Apply disclosure tier

Generate the review bundle aligned to authorized audience and policy-scoped redaction rules.

[STEP 05]

Draft summary

Produce a minimal incident narrative: what happened, when, scope, integrity status, and handling actions taken.

[STEP 06]

Package for counsel

Deliver the verified bundle and summary to internal counsel and designated reviewers under secure channel.

[TIME DISCIPLINE]
  • First 4 hours: identify scope, retrieve artifacts, run verification, preserve logs.
  • Next 24 hours: confirm disclosure tier, draft minimal narrative, conduct internal review.
  • Remainder: stakeholder coordination and required reporting per applicable policy and law.
[BOUNDARY]
  • This playbook provides operational guidance only and is not legal advice.
  • NADIR verifies artifact integrity and provenance under policy; it does not determine correctness or legality.
  • Reporting obligations and definitions of “serious incident” are determined by the operator’s legal and regulatory context.
